Sequencing rationale

Two foundational moves can begin immediately and independently: establishing a single authoritative source for credit standing (OPP1) and formalising ownership of the electronic channel (OPP2). Automated detection and recovery of failed electronic orders (OPP3) depends on OPP2, because alerts need a confirmed accountable owner and documented procedure to route into; building monitoring before ownership exists would leave failures detected but unowned. OPP3 is therefore scheduled after OPP2.

Dependencies: OPP3 depends on OPP2. OPP1 has no dependencies and runs in parallel.

Strategic readiness

The organisation already holds the data and documents needed to act: the two customer-data systems, the credit policy, the procedure and accountability matrix, and the order-flow analysis. The principal readiness constraint is governance rather than technology — assigning ownership and applying existing approval controls — which makes the foundational moves achievable quickly. The recovery automation requires modest build effort and benefits from the ownership groundwork laid first.
