Prioritization rationale

Each opportunity assessed across three readiness dimensions. The rating (high / medium / low) is followed by the reason, so the sequence is defensible rather than asserted.

Sequencing rationale

Two interventions stand alone and should start immediately: establishing a single trusted view of customer credit with controlled release, and bringing the electronic order channel into documented ownership. Both rely only on information already held and address the highest-impact findings. Recovering failed electronic orders depends on the channel first having a documented procedure and named owner, so it follows the ownership work rather than running ahead of it.

Dependencies: Only one genuine prerequisite exists: recovering failed electronic orders (OPP3) depends on the electronic channel being documented and owned (OPP2). The credit intervention (OPP1) has no dependencies.

Strategic readiness

The underlying information already exists in structured form across the credit, order, and escalation records, so the assessment rests on settled facts rather than new discovery. Readiness is strongest where the work is reconciliation and documentation, and is gated mainly by appointing named owners for credit approval and the electronic channel.
