Current State Assessment

How Order-to-Cash runs today. A factual baseline — no judgements.

Domain overview

This briefing examines the order-to-cash process for the European consumer-healthcare business: how customer orders are received, validated against credit controls, fulfilled, and how disputes are resolved. The process spans the central order-management system of record, the customer-relationship system, electronic ordering channels used by large retail customers, and the customer-service function that handles exceptions.

Orders reach the business through several channels, with electronic data interchange carrying the largest share. Each order is checked against the customer's credit standing before fulfilment. Credit limits and payment terms are held in both the central order-management system and the customer-relationship system. When orders fail to process or disputes arise, customer service intervenes. Formal procedure documents and an accountability matrix govern the manually handled order channels, while the electronic channel currently operates outside those formal documents and relies on a customer-service specialist's informal working notes.

Process inventory

  • Customer order capture — Receive customer orders across electronic and manual channels.
  • Customer master-data and credit setup — Establish and maintain credit limits and payment terms for retail customers in both systems of record.
  • Credit validation — Confirm each order is within the customer's authorised credit standing before fulfilment.
  • Order fulfilment — Process validated orders through to delivery.
  • Dispute and escalation resolution — Resolve orders that fail to process and customer disputes.
