Establish a single source of truth for customer credit standingAutomation Pipeline

Designate the central order-management system as the authoritative source for credit limits and payment terms, and introduce an automated reconciliation that flags and routes any disagreement between the two systems for review and written approval before it can take effect.

Before

1. Dual maintenance
Order-management and credit functions · Central order-management system and customer-relationship system
Credit limits and payment terms are entered and edited in both systems independently, with no reconciliation.
2. Order validated against whichever limit applies
Credit function · Both systems
Orders are validated against the limit in use, which for some large accounts is the higher relationship-system figure, bypassing written approval.

After

1. Authoritative source designated
Credit function · Central order-management system
The order-management system holds the approved credit limit and payment terms as the single source of truth.
2. Automated reconciliation and approval routing
Credit function with automated reconciliation · Both systems
Any disagreement between the two systems is automatically detected and routed for written approval under the policy's separation-of-duties controls before any change governs orders.

Business impact. Reconciling the two systems brings the 267 accounts with disagreeing credit limits and the 228 with disagreeing payment terms under a single authoritative figure, so the aggregate €30,675,000 difference is resolved and credit exposure on the largest accounts becomes assessable and policy-compliant. 267 accounts brought to a single authoritative credit limit   €30,675,000 aggregate credit-limit difference resolved   228 accounts brought to consistent payment terms

How it's delivered. Confirm the authoritative system, run a one-time reconciliation of the disagreeing accounts through the policy's approval controls, then stand up an ongoing automated comparison that prevents unapproved divergence going forward.

Connects: Central order-management system, Customer-relationship system

Success looks like:

  • Number of accounts with disagreeing credit limits reduced toward zero
  • All credit-limit changes carry written approval under policy
  • Aggregate credit-limit difference eliminated

Dependencies: Independent — can start immediately.

Risks:

  • Resolving overstated limits may tighten credit headroom for some large accounts and require commercial conversations

Where this comes from: your Credit Management Policy Europe and your Accounts Receivable Review Notes Q4 2025

Formalise ownership and procedure for the electronic order channelHITL Workflow

Bring the electronic channel into the formal procedure and accountability matrix: assign a named Accountable owner, document the end-to-end handling and escalation steps, and replace the single specialist's informal notes with an authoritative, controlled procedure.

Before

1. Channel excluded from procedure
Order-management function · Electronic ordering channel
The electronic channel, carrying 67.3% of volume, is excluded from the procedure and accountability matrix with no Accountable owner.
2. Disputes handled by informal notes
Single customer-service specialist · Customer-service handling
Escalations are resolved using one person's informal working notes.

After

1. Accountable owner assigned
Order-management leadership · Electronic ordering channel
A named owner is made Accountable for the electronic channel within the accountability matrix.
2. Authoritative procedure and escalation path
Order-management and customer-service functions · Electronic ordering channel and customer-service handling
The procedure is extended to cover the electronic channel end to end, with a documented escalation path replacing the informal notes.

Business impact. Bringing the channel that carries 67.3% of volume and €59,711,399.53 of order value under formal ownership gives escalation authority and controls to the majority of the business, and provides a documented basis for tackling the 34 order-not-processed escalations that make up 23.9% of all escalations. €59,711,399.53 of order value brought under formal accountability   67.3% of order volume gains an authoritative procedure   34 order-not-processed escalations gain a documented owner and path

How it's delivered. Capture the specialist's working knowledge into a formal procedure, assign an Accountable owner in the matrix, define escalation authority, and socialise the procedure across order-management and customer-service teams.

Connects: Electronic ordering channel, Customer-service handling

Success looks like:

  • Electronic channel has a named Accountable owner in the matrix
  • Documented procedure replaces informal notes
  • Reduction in order-not-processed escalations

Dependencies: Independent — can start immediately. Prerequisite for the AI Credit Decisioning step.

Risks:

  • Knowledge concentrated in one individual may be incompletely captured if that person is unavailable

Where this comes from: your Order Management SOP Europe, your O2C Process RACI Europe and your EDI Dispute Resolution CS Working Notes

Automated monitoring and recovery of failed electronic ordersAutomation Pipeline

Introduce automated detection of electronic orders that fail to transmit or process, with alerts routed to the newly accountable owner for prompt recovery, so failures are caught before customers escalate or threaten delisting.

Before

1. Failures surface via customer complaints
Customer-service function · Customer-service handling
Failed orders are typically discovered when customers escalate, by which point relationships are already strained.
2. No systematic recovery
Order-management function · Central order-management system
1,196 electronic orders worth €12,362,493.74 went unfulfilled without a systematic catch-and-recover step.

After

1. Automated failure detection
Automated monitoring · Electronic ordering channel and central order-management system
Electronic orders that fail to transmit or process are detected automatically and flagged in real time.
2. Owner-led recovery
Accountable electronic-channel owner · Customer-service handling
Alerts route to the accountable owner who drives recovery before the customer is affected.

Business impact. Catching failures proactively targets the 1,196 unfulfilled electronic orders worth €12,362,493.74 — the bulk of the 1,667 total unfulfilled orders worth €17,377,801.40 — protecting revenue and the customer relationships at risk of delisting. 1,196 unfulfilled electronic orders targeted for recovery   €12,362,493.74 of electronic order value at stake   €17,377,801.40 total unfulfilled value across all channels

How it's delivered. Define what a failed order looks like, build automated detection against electronic-channel transmissions, and route alerts to the accountable owner with a documented recovery procedure — building on the ownership established in OPP2.

Connects: Electronic ordering channel, Central order-management system, Customer-service handling

Success looks like:

  • Failed electronic orders detected before customer escalation
  • Reduction in unfulfilled electronic order count and value
  • Reduction in delisting threats

Dependencies: Requires Customer Master Reconciliation first.

Risks:

  • Without a confirmed owner from OPP2, alerts have no destination and recovery stalls

Where this comes from: your Order Flow Analysis Export 2025 and your EDI Dispute Resolution CS Working Notes
