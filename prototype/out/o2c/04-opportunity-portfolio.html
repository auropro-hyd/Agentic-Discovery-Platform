Single trusted view of customer credit, with controlled releaseHITL Workflow

Establish one authoritative credit limit and payment terms per customer aligned to the policy's system of record, surface every divergence for review, and route any order that would release above the authoritative limit to a credit owner for approval before it proceeds.

Before

1. Limit read at release
Credit team · Two systems
An order is checked against whichever credit limit is read at release time — sometimes the commercial view, sometimes the system of record.
2. Silent over-release
Credit team · Two systems
Where the commercial limit is higher, the order releases above the system of record with no approval trail.

After

1. Reconcile to one source
Master-data and credit teams · Core enterprise resource planning system
Each customer's credit limit and payment terms are reconciled to the policy's system of record, and every divergence is listed for review and decision.
2. Controlled exception release
Credit owner · Core enterprise resource planning system
Any order that would release above the authoritative limit is held and routed to a named credit owner for approval, creating an approval trail.

Business impact. Brings the 30675000 EUR of aggregate divergence under review and replaces silent over-release with a controlled, traceable approval, protecting the largest retail accounts where deltas reach 600000 EUR. 30675000 EUR of divergence brought under review   267 customers with mismatched limits reconciled   228 payment-term mismatches resolved

How we get there: Figures restate the verified divergence and mismatch counts.

How it's delivered. Confirm the policy's system of record as authoritative, reconcile each customer record to it, present every divergence for a documented decision, and introduce an approval step that holds any above-limit release for a named credit owner.

Who uses it. Credit owner, Master-data team, Order management team

Expected behaviour. Day to day it shows one authoritative limit per customer and holds any order that would exceed it for approval; it never auto-approves an above-limit release on its own.

Escalation & human fallback. Any above-limit release, and any divergence it cannot reconcile automatically, is handed to the named credit owner for a documented decision.

Dependencies: Independent — can start immediately.

Where this comes from: Credit Management Policy Europe, Accounts Receivable Review Notes Q4 2025, SAP CRM Customer Export and SAP S/4HANA Customer Master Export

Bring the electronic order channel into documented ownershipModernisation

Write the electronic channel into the order management procedure and the responsibility matrix, assign a named accountable owner, and convert the informal dispute notes into a documented resolution procedure.

Before

1. Unowned channel
Not assigned · Electronic data interchange channel
Two-thirds of orders run through a channel absent from the procedure and the responsibility matrix, with no accountable owner.
2. Informal resolution
Customer service team · Informal working notes
Electronic-order disputes are worked from informal notes rather than a documented procedure.

After

1. Documented and owned
Process owner · Order management procedure and responsibility matrix
The electronic channel is added to the procedure and the responsibility matrix with a named accountable owner.
2. Standard resolution path
Customer service team · Documented resolution procedure
Electronic-order disputes follow a documented procedure with a clear owner and escalation route.

Business impact. Brings the channel carrying 67.3 percent of orders and 59711399.53 EUR of value under documented ownership and gives the most frequent escalation — 34 cases, 23.9 percent of all escalations — a clear owner and procedure. 67.3 percent of orders brought under documented ownership   59711399.53 EUR of order value covered   34 manual-intervention escalations given a clear owner

How we get there: Figures restate the verified channel share, value, and escalation counts.

How it's delivered. Document the electronic channel end to end in the order management procedure, assign accountability in the responsibility matrix, and formalise the dispute-resolution notes into a standard procedure.

Who uses it. Process owner, Customer service team, Order management team

Expected behaviour. Day to day it gives the electronic channel a written procedure and a named owner for every step; it never leaves an electronic dispute without a defined resolution path.

Escalation & human fallback. Disputes that fall outside the documented procedure are escalated to the named channel owner.

Dependencies: Independent — can start immediately. Prerequisite for the AI Credit Decisioning step.

Where this comes from: Order Flow Analysis Export 2025, Order Management SOP Europe, O2C Process RACI Europe and EDI Dispute Resolution CS Working Notes

Catch and recover failed electronic orders before they become complaintsAutomation Pipeline

Monitor electronic orders for those that need manual re-entry or fall out of fulfilment, route them automatically to the owning team for completion, and recover them before they surface as customer escalations or delisting threats.

Before

1. Silent fall-out
Not assigned · Electronic data interchange channel
Electronic orders that need manual re-entry quietly fall out of fulfilment, accumulating as 1196 unfulfilled orders worth 12362493.74 EUR.
2. Surface as complaints
Customer service team · Customer service records
Failures surface only as stockout and 'order not received' escalations and delisting threats.

After

1. Detect and route
Order management team · Core enterprise resource planning system
Electronic orders needing manual intervention are detected and routed automatically to the owning team for completion.
2. Recover before complaint
Order management team · Core enterprise resource planning system
Failed orders are completed before they reach the customer as a missed delivery, reducing escalations.

Business impact. Targets the 1196 unfulfilled electronic orders worth 12362493.74 EUR — far above the 320 manual and 111 email equivalents — recovering revenue and protecting key retail relationships. 1196 unfulfilled electronic orders targeted for recovery   12362493.74 EUR of unfulfilled value addressed   320 unfulfilled manual orders by comparison

How we get there: Figures restate the verified unfulfilled counts and value.

How it's delivered. Introduce monitoring of electronic orders for failure to process or fulfil, route exceptions to the owning team automatically, and track recovery against the verified unfulfilled baseline.

Who uses it. Order management team, Customer service team, Channel owner

Expected behaviour. Day to day it watches for failed electronic orders and routes them for completion; it never completes or amends an order on its own without the owning team acting on it.

Escalation & human fallback. Orders it cannot route or that remain incomplete past a threshold are handed to the named channel owner.

Dependencies: Requires Customer Master Reconciliation first.

Where this comes from: Order Flow Analysis Export 2025, Customer Service Escalation Log 2025, EDI Dispute Resolution CS Working Notes and EDI Integration Register Europe
