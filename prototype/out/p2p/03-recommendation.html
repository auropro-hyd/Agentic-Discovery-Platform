Sequencing rationale

The enforced second approval is the foundation: it directly protects the largest share of value (EUR 557,000, 91.9% of total) and nothing else depends on it being delayed, so it goes first. The order-first control builds on that enforcement, because the EUR 216,000 committed before an order existed is the same value that also lacked a second approval; closing the sequencing path on top of enforced approval gives the strongest combined effect, so it follows. The named owner and monitoring layer comes last because it watches over both enforced controls and is only as effective as the rules it monitors.

Dependencies: OPP2 depends on OPP1 because the order-first control is most effective once second approval is enforced on the same high-value orders. OPP3 depends on both OPP1 and OPP2 because monitoring is meaningful only once the controls it oversees are enforced.

Strategic readiness

The controls already exist in policy; the work is to enforce them in the working process and assign accountability. This is a high-readiness programme because the rules and the evidence are settled, and the first move is both the highest value and the most feasible.
