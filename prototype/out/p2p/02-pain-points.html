Pain Points & Opportunities

The issues found in the discovery, ranked by business impact, each mapped to a recommended opportunity.

Largest orders committed without the required second approval

Every purchase order above the EUR 50,000 dual-approval threshold received only a single approval. This accounts for 7 orders representing EUR 557,000 of committed spend, which is 91.9% of the total value reviewed.

7 orders received only a single approval   EUR 557,000 of committed spend   91.9% of total value reviewed

Root cause: The approval threshold exists in the policy but is not enforced at the point an order is committed, so a second approval can be skipped without anything stopping the commitment.

Pattern: Control defined in policy but not enforced in the working process.

Addressed by: OPP1 (see the Opportunity Portfolio)

Where this comes from: your Procurement Policy and your Purchase Order Export

Spend committed to suppliers before any purchase order exists

Spend banned by policy occurs on 25.0% of orders, worth EUR 216,000, where commitments were made to suppliers before a purchase order existed. All 3 of these orders also received only a single approval, with the full EUR 216,000 affected by both issues at once.

3 orders committed before a purchase order existed   25.0% of orders   EUR 216,000 committed without a prior order

Root cause: The policy rule requiring an order before any supplier commitment is not built into the working process, so buyers can commit first and document later.

Pattern: Sequencing control bypassed, compounding with the missing second approval on the same orders.

Addressed by: OPP3 (see the Opportunity Portfolio)

Where this comes from: your Procurement Policy and your Purchase Order Export

Repeat non-compliance concentrated in one buyer, with no owner

Repeat non-compliance is concentrated in a single buyer, accounting for 2 orders worth EUR 149,000, which is 24.6% of value. No document assigns anyone to own, monitor, or grant exceptions to the control, so the pattern goes undetected and unremediated.

2 orders from a single buyer   EUR 149,000 concentrated in one buyer   24.6% of value

Root cause: No accountable owner is assigned for monitoring control adherence, so concentrated non-compliance is never surfaced or addressed.

Pattern: Unowned control with no monitoring, allowing repeat behaviour to persist.

Addressed by: OPP2 (see the Opportunity Portfolio)

Where this comes from: your Purchase Order Export and your Procurement Policy
