Current State Assessment

How Procure-to-Pay runs today at Acme Manufacturing. A factual baseline — no judgements.

Domain overview

This assessment covers how the company commits spend with suppliers through its purchase order process, from the point a buyer raises an order to the point that order is approved and committed. The review draws on the company's procurement policy and a recent export of purchase order records.

Buyers raise purchase orders against supplier requirements and submit them for approval. The procurement policy sets the rules for when an order may be committed, including an approval threshold above which a second approver is required, and a sequencing rule requiring a purchase order to exist before any commitment is made to a supplier. In the records reviewed, 7 of the purchase orders received only a single approval, representing 58.3% of orders and EUR 557,000 of committed spend. A further 3 orders, representing 25.0% of orders and EUR 216,000 of value, were committed to suppliers before a purchase order existed.
