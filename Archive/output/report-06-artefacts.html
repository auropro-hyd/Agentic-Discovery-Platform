Artefact H — Decision Rationale Register
Why Each Recommendation — Evidence Basis & Alternatives Rejected
For each of the five recommendations and two AI use cases, this register records the primary evidence basis, the specific decision made, and the most significant alternative that was considered and rejected.
IDDecisionEvidence BasisAlternative ConsideredWhy Rejected
R-01 Mandate SAP S/4HANA as sole credit data SOR; OPP-01 AI weekly reconciliation Policy §4.1 already mandates ERP. 8 accounts diverged. €2.25M overstatement. No existing automated check. Manual monthly reconciliation by Credit Controller (no AI) Weekly manual export-and-compare takes ~4h per run. At scale (340 accounts), monthly cadence is too slow — a new discrepancy could persist for 30 days. AI pipeline runs in <10 min weekly with no marginal effort.
R-02 Author EDI SOP as a Wave 1 Quick Win (Weeks 1–4, no IT dependency) Order Mgmt SOP §1.3 explicitly excludes EDI. RACI has no EDI row. Alina Kovacs's working notes are the only procedure — not official, not accessible to new staff. Wait for OPP-04 monitoring to be live before formalising the SOP (so the SOP could reference alert system) Monitoring without documented procedure creates a circular dependency: alerts fire but CS team has no authoritative reference for how to respond. SOP must precede tooling so the tool enforces the process, not replaces it.
R-03 Phased TSA migration — Carrefour FR first, Boots UK second, remainder in H3 34 EDI incidents; "third time this quarter for Carrefour FR." Delist threat from Boots UK. 24–48h Sanofi SLA vs same-day Opella Digital SLA. Negotiate a bulk handover of all 6 connections simultaneously Bulk handover requires Sanofi cooperation on 6 parallel migration workstreams. Risk of any single failure delaying all others. Phased approach delivers value from the highest-risk connection first; each successful handover de-risks the next.
R-04 Deploy OPP-04 EDI monitoring with TSA-aware routing built in from Week 6 59% fulfilment rate in seasonal windows. 19 stockout complaints. Detection currently reactive (customer complaints). Use a third-party EDI monitoring SaaS tool (e.g. OpenText, SPS Commerce) Third-party SaaS would not natively distinguish between Opella Digital and Sanofi IT-managed connections, requiring custom configuration. Building OPP-04 on top of existing event data allows exact routing logic without new vendor contract or data egress.
R-05 Dedup customer master: add 22 ERP-only accounts to CRM; consolidate 6 duplicates 340 ERP vs 318 CRM accounts. 6 top-8 accounts show Sanofi legacy migration source. Duplicate records identified in Doc 09 source field. Rebuild CRM from ERP as a full data migration Full migration is H3-scale work (weeks of IT effort; UAT required). The gap of 22 accounts and 6 duplicates is a targeted remediation achievable in weeks. Full migration should follow after R-01 enforces ERP as SOR — not precede it.
OPP-01 LLM-powered classification (cause: migration artifact / AM override / drift) with deterministic join step Discrepancy causes are not mechanically determinable from field values alone — need reasoning across migration_source, last_updated_by, and policy rules. Rule-based classifier (if migration_source=Sanofi then "migration artifact") Rule-based misses cases where an AM subsequently overrode a migration discrepancy (mixed cause). LLM reads the full account context and applies the policy, producing more nuanced and useful recommendations.
OPP-04 5-pattern anomaly detection with TSA-aware routing; human-in-the-loop H1 34 incidents decompose into 5 distinct failure modes in the CS log. TSA routing is the single most consequential routing decision — mis-routing to Opella Digital for Sanofi connection costs hours. Simple threshold alert on fulfilment rate only (no pattern library) Fulfilment rate alone detects degradation too late — 7-day rolling window means a full connection outage runs for days before the rate threshold is breached. Pattern library detects failure modes within 30 minutes of the triggering event.
