Sequencing Principles
Three principles govern the ordering of all workstreams in this roadmap:

1. Governance before automation. R-02 (EDI SOP) and R-01 (credit data authority) must establish clear rules before AI tools (OPP-01, OPP-04) operate on that data. An AI tool that enforces the wrong rule is worse than no AI tool.

2. Quick wins fund credibility. R-02 (EDI SOP, weeks 1–4) and OPP-04 MVP (weeks 3–6) deliver visible results early. These create the organisational confidence that makes the heavier H2 and H3 investments easier to approve.

3. TSA constraints are non-negotiable. R-03 (TSA Migration) is phased around Sanofi cooperation and contractual timelines — not around Opella's preferred delivery speed. No workstream can force the TSA handover faster than the TSA agreement allows.
Dependency Shorthand Used in This Report

Enables: workstream A must be at least partially complete before workstream B can begin its gate-locked phase. Informs: workstream A produces an artefact (data, document, rule) that improves workstream B's accuracy or scope, but B can proceed without waiting. Blocks: workstream B cannot proceed at all until workstream A is complete.

§1 Roadmap Overview
The O2C transformation programme addresses the consequences of Opella's April 2025 carve-out from Sanofi across three time horizons. H1 is a foundation sprint that establishes governance, launches AI tooling in human-supervised mode, and closes the most acute data quality gaps. H2 stabilises the improvements — refining AI accuracy, completing the customer master, and initiating the first TSA connection handovers. H3 completes the structural transformation: the remaining TSA connections are migrated, SAP CRM is rationalised against ERP, and AI tooling is assessed for supervised automation where governance evidence supports it.

§2 Now — H1 Foundation Sprint (Months 0–6)
H1 is the most intensive period of the programme. Five workstreams run concurrently at peak (months 2–4). The sequencing is deliberate: R-02 launches first because it has no dependencies and delivers immediate value; OPP-04 and OPP-01 launch on staggered 6-week cycles to spread IT integration demand; R-05 runs in parallel because it requires only data exports, not system access; R-01 begins in month 3 once OPP-01 has produced its first reconciliation report and identified the full discrepancy set.

§3 Next — H2 Stabilisation (Months 6–18)
H2 converts H1 foundations into durable operating capability. The governance structures established in H1 are tested and refined. AI accuracy is formally assessed. The first TSA connection handovers begin — these are the highest-risk activities in the programme because they depend on Sanofi cooperation. H2 ends with Opella in control of at least two of the six Sanofi-managed EDI connections.
H2 Workstreams — Months 6–18
Continuing from H1 + new TSA handover activity
R-01
Credit Data Authority — Full enforcement + automated pipeline
Phases 2–4 of R-01: expand reconciliation from top-4 to all 340 accounts; implement automated data sync between ERP and CRM (coordinated with Opella Digital); Credit Controller pipeline review becomes monthly rather than weekly for low-risk accounts. Milestone: zero manual credit decisions based on CRM-only data by month 12.
OPP-01
Credit Engine Mature — 3-month accuracy review + CRM auto-update proposal
Formal accuracy review at month 9: if ≥95% classification accuracy sustained over 3 consecutive months, prepare business case for supervised CRM auto-update (the H2 automation gate from Report 04 §6). Marc Dupont and Raj Patel review. Legal/compliance review of FMCH requirements. Auto-update proposal is a proposal — not a commitment.
OPP-04
EDI Monitoring Mature — Apr–May seasonal window + escalation log mining
April–May is the first full seasonal window after OPP-04 Full deployment. Threshold tightening protocol active (85% fulfilment rate floor). Escalation log mined for TSA migration evidence: all Sanofi-managed connection escalations since deployment compiled into a negotiation dossier for James Okafor's TSA review meetings.
R-03
TSA Phase 1 — Carrefour France handover
Carrefour France is the highest-priority connection (overdue per Report 02; "third time this quarter" escalation in CS log). Handover transfers EDI connection management from Sanofi IT helpdesk to Opella Digital. James Okafor leads technical migration. Same-day response replaces 24–48h Sanofi SLA. Post-handover: 90-day stabilisation period before declaring success.
R-03
TSA Phase 2 — Boots UK legacy decommission
Boots UK decommissions the legacy Sanofi EDI connection and establishes a direct Opella Digital connection. Boots UK is the second-largest account by credit limit (€1.2M ERP) and has recorded stockout and delist threat escalations. Phase 2 target: month 15, subject to Sanofi cooperation on Annex C handover terms.
R-01 + R-02
SOP & credit governance audit
6-month post-publication SOP effectiveness review (month 10): how many EDI incidents were resolved using the SOP? How many still required ad-hoc escalation? Outputs feed into SOP v2.0. Credit policy compliance audit: are all credit hold releases documented? Are all limit changes logged in the credit management log?
H2 End State — What "Done" Looks Like
  • All 340 accounts have a consistent ERP = CRM credit record; automated sync prevents recurrence (R-01 complete)
  • OPP-01 accuracy formally assessed; CRM auto-update proposal submitted or deferred with rationale (OPP-01 mature)
  • Apr–May seasonal window managed with ≥91% EDI fulfilment rate; escalation dossier delivered to TSA programme (OPP-04 mature)
  • Carrefour France EDI connection managed by Opella Digital (R-03 Phase 1 complete)
  • Boots UK migration in progress or complete (R-03 Phase 2 underway)
  • SOP v2.0 published following audit; credit governance audit complete (R-01 + R-02 audit)

§4 Later — H3 Transformation (Months 18–36+)
H3 is the structural transformation horizon. The governance and AI tooling established in H1–H2 are mature enough to support more ambitious changes: completing the TSA migration (4 remaining connections), rationalising the SAP CRM architecture, and potentially enabling supervised AI automation where the H2 gating criteria are met. H3 is the least certain horizon — timelines depend on Sanofi's TSA cooperation, Opella's technology investment decisions, and the AI accuracy evidence from H2.
H3 Workstreams — Months 18–36+
Contingent on H2 outcomes · Sanofi TSA cooperation required
R-03
TSA Phase 3 — E.Leclerc, dm, Lidl Europe, Coop Group
The four remaining Sanofi-managed connections migrate to Opella Digital in priority order: E.Leclerc (France, proximity to Carrefour FR infrastructure), dm (DACH cluster), Lidl Europe and Coop Group together (EU generic). Full TSA migration completion target: Q3 2026 (month 24). At completion: all 14 EDI connections managed by Opella Digital — no Sanofi dependency for EDI operations.
R-01
SAP CRM rationalisation decision
By month 18, the credit data authority is fully enforced via ERP. The SAP CRM system's credit-related fields are either (a) reliably auto-synced from ERP (lower cost, lower risk), or (b) candidates for removal if OPP-01 automation is approved. Technology leadership (James Okafor + Marc Dupont) makes the rationalisation decision based on H2 AI accuracy evidence and IT cost analysis. This is a decision gate — not a committed workstream.
OPP-01 + OPP-04
AI supervised automation assessment
If both OPP-01 and OPP-04 have met the H2 gating criteria (≥95% accuracy over 3+ months; business owner sign-off; FMCH compliance review complete), H3 scopes supervised automation: OPP-01 writes CRM updates directly (Credit Controller reviews exceptions only); OPP-04 dispatches Sanofi helpdesk tickets automatically for P2/P3 alerts (CS team reviews P1 only). Scope is narrow and reversible. Full automation remains out of scope.

  • SAP CRM credit data rationalised — all records auto-synced from ERP or credit fields removed (R-01 + CRM decision)
  • AI automation scope approved (or formally deferred) based on H2 accuracy evidence (OPP-01 + OPP-04 gate)
  • O2C domain operates without carve-out inheritance debt — no Sanofi-era workarounds, no undocumented processes

§5 Master Dependency Map
Nine dependency relationships govern the sequencing of the seven workstreams. Five are hard enables (one workstream must reach a specific milestone before another can proceed to its gate-locked phase). Four are soft informs (one workstream's output improves another's accuracy or scope, but does not block it).

Critical Path
The critical path is: Data access (W1–2) → OPP-01 MVP (W8) → R-01 Phase 1 (M3) → R-01 Full (H2 M12) → H2 AI Gate (M15) → H3 automation scope (M18). Any slip in Raj Patel's capacity in weeks 8–12 cascades through R-01 into the H2 AI gate. The TSA programme has its own parallel critical path governed by Sanofi cooperation, not Opella delivery speed.
