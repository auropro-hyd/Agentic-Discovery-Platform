§1 Transformation Intent Opella Europe · Order-to-Cash · CONFIDENTIAL
§1   Transformation Intent
1.1   Strategic Framing

The five pain points identified in RPT-OE-O2C-02 are not isolated operational failures — they are the compounding consequence of a single structural event: the April 2025 carve-out from Sanofi. The transformation programme recommended in this report has one governing objective: eliminate Opella Europe's O2C carve-out inheritance debt by the end of H2 2026, and build the data, process, and infrastructure independence required to sustain a modernised O2C operating model.

The locked O2C Strategy Profile (Direction: Consolidate + Modernise) anchors the approach. Consolidation means resolving the dual-system data problem — one authoritative source for credit limits, one complete customer master, one governed set of process documents. Modernisation means deploying automation and AI to enforce governance at scale, monitor EDI channel health in real time, and reduce the manual overhead that currently absorbs CS team capacity. The recommendations in this report are sequenced so that consolidation work in H1 creates the stable foundation on which modernisation in H2 can build.

1.2   Guiding Principles
P1
ERP is the Single Source of Truth
SAP S/4HANA is the authoritative system for all credit data. No recommendation introduces a competing authority. All data actions converge on ERP accuracy.
P2
Governance Before Automation
Automate only what is first governed. The EDI SOP and credit governance workflow must be in place before AI-powered monitoring or reconciliation is deployed against them.
P3
TSA Constraints are Non-Negotiable
TSA migration pace is governed by the TSA Programme Office and Sanofi cooperation. Recommendations do not assume accelerated Sanofi response; they sequence around realistic constraints.
P4
Quick Wins Fund Credibility
R-02 (EDI SOP, weeks) and top-account credit reconciliation (days) deliver immediate governance closure. Early visible wins create organisational momentum for the longer-horizon programme items.
1.3   Scope Boundaries

This recommendation covers the five pain points in scope of the O2C domain discovery. It does not address: (a) supply chain planning or demand sensing — the cross-domain root cause of the April–May and October–November stockout pattern (flagged for separate investigation); (b) application rationalisation decisions beyond the current SAP estate — these are H3 horizon items subject to a full portfolio review; (c) commercial pricing, contracting, or account management process — these are outside the O2C boundary as defined in RPT-OE-O2C-01.

R
01
Establish SAP S/4HANA as Sole Credit Data Authority
Critical Priority H1 — 0–6 Months AI Opportunity Addresses PP-01, PP-05  |  Delivers OPP-01, OPP-05

The Credit Management Policy (OPS-EU-FIN-POL-002, §4.1) is unambiguous: SAP S/4HANA is the single authoritative system of record for all customer credit limits. The four-account discrepancy identified in RPT-OE-O2C-02 — with total CRM overstatement of €1,450,000 — represents active credit exposure above the policy-approved limit. This recommendation closes the existing gap, establishes governance controls to prevent recurrence, and deploys an automated reconciliation pipeline to sustain compliance at scale.

Phased Actions
  • H1 · Week 1–2 Credit Controller (Raj Patel) to complete immediate reconciliation of the four known discrepant accounts — Carrefour France, Boots UK, E.Leclerc, dm (Drogerie Markt). For each account: confirm the ERP S/4HANA limit as authoritative; obtain Finance Director (Marc Dupont) written approval for any required ERP adjustment; update CRM to match ERP; document the remediation in the credit management log as required by §4.2.
  • H1 · Month 1–2 Extend reconciliation to the top 50 revenue accounts — as recommended by Raj Patel in the Q4 2025 AR Review. Implement interim governance rule: any CRM credit limit field update requires a Credit Controller email approval record before the change takes effect. CS Team Lead to communicate this to the CS team and Account Managers with explicit reference to the policy breach consequence defined in OPS-EU-FIN-POL-002, §5.3.
  • H1 · Month 2–4 Resolve the Boots UK payment terms conflict (ERP: NET45, CRM: NET30). Commercial team and Credit Controller to confirm the contractually agreed terms; update both systems; notify the Boots UK account manager. Conduct a systematic payment terms review for all accounts where ERP and CRM terms differ.
  • H2 · Month 4–8 Deploy automated ERP/CRM credit reconciliation pipeline (OPP-01): scheduled nightly comparison of credit limits and payment terms; materiality threshold alerts (>€50K variance or any terms mismatch) routed to Credit Controller queue; automated CRM correction workflow with mandatory Credit Controller approval token before update executes. AI assists in resolving ambiguous cases by surfacing approval history, audit trail patterns, and correspondence.
Success Criteria
Zero ERP/CRM credit discrepancies >€50K for top 50 accounts by end H1 100% of CRM credit updates carry documented Credit Controller approval Automated reconciliation pipeline live by end H2 Zero payment terms conflicts for active accounts
Dependency
R-01 is a prerequisite for R-05 (Customer Master Deduplication). The ERP/CRM governance framework established under R-01 — specifically the CRM update approval workflow — is the control mechanism that R-05 relies on to prevent new data divergence after the deduplication exercise.

R
02
Author and Publish the EDI SOP and RACI Addendum
Critical Priority Quick Win — H1 Weeks Addresses PP-02  |  Delivers OPP-02

This is the highest-impact, lowest-effort recommendation in the programme. The Q3 2025 commitment to publish an EDI SOP and RACI addendum — made in both OPS-EU-CS-SOP-001 (§1.3) and OPS-EU-CS-RACI-001 (version history) — remains unfulfilled as of November 2025. The materials needed to author these documents are already in the discovery pack: the CS Working Notes (OPS-EU-IT-WN-001) provide the complete operational procedure; the Sanofi SOP v4.0 §11 provides the structural template; the EDI Integration Register (OPS-EU-IT-REG-001) provides the connection-specific technical detail. No new process design is required — only documentation and governance.

Phased Actions
  • H1 · Week 1–3 Draft the EDI Order Management SOP using: (1) Alina Kovacs's working notes (OPS-EU-IT-WN-001) as the primary procedural source — these are comprehensive and operationally accurate; (2) Sanofi SOP v4.0, §11 (EDI) as structural reference for section headings and format; (3) EDI Integration Register §3–§5 for connection-specific escalation paths and SLA tables. AI drafting can compress this to 1–2 weeks of effort for an initial draft. The SOP must cover: EDI order receipt and SAP integration; credit hold interaction on EDI orders; duplicate order detection and resolution; mapping error handling; TSA-managed connection escalation protocol; Sanofi IT helpdesk contact and P1/P2 definitions.
  • H1 · Week 3–4 CS Team Lead (Alina Kovacs) review and amendment; Opella Digital (James Okafor) technical accuracy review for EDI platform specifics and TSA governance process. Resolve any discrepancies between working notes and the EDI register's governance requirements.
  • H1 · Week 4–5 Finance Director and VP Commercial Operations approval. Publish as the EDI addendum to OPS-EU-CS-SOP-001 (reference: OPS-EU-CS-SOP-001-EDI v1.0). Simultaneously update the RACI Matrix (OPS-EU-CS-RACI-001) with EDI-specific accountability rows for: EDI order monitoring, EDI incident escalation (Opella Digital-managed), EDI incident escalation (TSA-managed), credit hold resolution for EDI orders, and EDI-specific monthly reporting.
  • H1 · Week 5–6 Brief all CS Representatives and Account Managers on the published SOP. Retire OPS-EU-IT-WN-001 as the primary EDI reference (it may be retained as supplementary background but must not be used as the operational procedure). Confirm James Okafor has reviewed and accepted the TSA escalation protocol as documented.

R
03
Execute the TSA EDI Connection Migration Programme
High Priority H1 → H3 Programme Strategic Programme Addresses PP-03, PP-04  |  Delivers OPP-03

Six of Opella's 14 EDI connections remain under Sanofi Shared Services management, creating a 24–48 hour incident resolution SLA dependency on five of the domain's highest-revenue accounts. The migration programme is a multi-quarter strategic commitment — it is the only recommendation in this report that cannot be completed within H1 — but it must begin in H1 to meet the EDI Register's existing (and already-missed) target dates. The Carrefour France connection (#9) had a Q4 2025 migration target; it is already overdue.

Phased Actions
  • H1 · Month 1 Governance foundation: Obtain and formally review TSA Schedule Annex C from the Opella TSA Programme Office. Document the contractual SLAs, notice requirements, and technical handover obligations for each of the 6 connections. Incorporate Annex C terms into the O2C operational risk register. Without Annex C, migration planning cannot be reliably sequenced against contractual obligations.
  • H1 · Month 1–3 Assign leads and formalise the programme: Appoint named migration leads for all six connections (connections 12–14 currently show "TBD"). James Okafor (EDI Integration Lead) to lead connections 9–11; identify resource for connections 12–14, which involve volume complexity (E.Leclerc) and multi-country routing (Lidl Europe). Establish a quarterly migration review with the TSA Programme Office.
  • H1 · Month 2–6 Carrefour France (#9) — priority migration: Complete technical scoping and begin migration to the Opella Digital platform (IBM Sterling B2B Integrator, Frankfurt). This is the highest-revenue connection on the longest-overdue target. Full ORDERS/ORDRSP/INVOIC message type migration. Target: go-live on Opella Digital management by end of H1.
  • H2 · Month 6–12 Boots UK legacy (#10) and dm (#11): Boots UK legacy decommission is straightforward — new Boots UK connection (#4) is already live on Opella Digital (since August 2025). Formal decommission of legacy connection and TSA exit. dm (Drogerie Markt) pre-scoping to begin in H1; full migration targeting H2.
  • H3 · Month 12–18+ E.Leclerc (#12), Lidl Europe (#13), Coop Group legacy (#14): These three require dedicated resource allocation and more complex technical scoping. E.Leclerc is the highest volume complexity; Lidl involves multi-country routing. Coop Group legacy decommission follows new Coop connection (#8). Target: all six connections migrated by Q3 2026 as committed in the EDI Register.

R
04
Deploy EDI Monitoring and Seasonal Coverage Protocol
Medium Priority H1 — Enabled by R-02 AI Opportunity Addresses PP-04  |  Delivers OPP-04

Order flow analysis identified a systematic EDI fulfilment rate degradation to ~59% in April–May and October–November 2025 — the two peak demand windows in the FMCG calendar. The 19 stockout customer complaints in the same periods represent an active customer relationship risk. This recommendation deploys proactive monitoring on the Opella Digital-managed connections and establishes a seasonal coverage protocol to prevent fulfilment degradation before it reaches the customer.

  • H1 · Month 1–2Configure EDI health monitoring alerts on the Opella Digital platform (IBM Sterling) for connections 1–8. Alert thresholds: EDI fulfilment rate <85% over any rolling 48-hour window; ORDRSP timeout rate >5% on any single connection; any connection with zero ORDERS received for >4 hours during business hours.
  • H1 · Month 2Define and publish the Seasonal EDI Coverage Protocol: for the April–May and October–November windows, activate manual order backup pre-authorisation for the five highest-EDI-volume accounts (Carrefour France, E.Leclerc, Boots UK, dm, Lidl); set daily EDI health check cadence (vs. reactive-only); CS Team Lead to hold a pre-season briefing with CS team and James Okafor before each window.
  • H2 · Month 6–9Integrate AI anomaly detection (OPP-04): train on 2025 order flow data to identify early indicators of fulfilment rate decline (mapping error frequency, ORDRSP timeout clustering, specific account order volume drops). Alert the CS Team Lead 24–48 hours before fulfilment rate reaches the alert threshold, enabling proactive intervention. Note: TSA-managed connection monitoring (connections 9–14) requires Sanofi platform cooperation; implement as each connection migrates to Opella Digital under R-03.
EDI fulfilment ≥ 85% in all calendar windows (including Apr–May, Oct–Nov) Customer stockout escalations <5 per quarter AI anomaly detection live on Opella Digital connections by end H2

05
Execute Customer Master Deduplication and Governance
Medium Priority H1 — Month 1–3 Addresses PP-05  |  Delivers OPP-05  |  Requires R-01 governance framework

SAP S/4HANA holds 340 active customer records; SAP CRM holds 318 — a gap of 22 accounts that exist only in ERP. Six Sanofi-migrated accounts carry active records in both systems with inconsistent data. The root cause — two live customer datasets inherited from Sanofi at carve-out — will not self-correct; it requires a deliberate deduplication exercise anchored on the ERP as authoritative, followed by the governance controls established under R-01 to prevent recurrence.

  • H1 · Month 1–2Extract the 22 ERP-only accounts. For each: determine whether CRM is used in commercial operations for that account; where yes, create the CRM record populated from ERP data; where no, document the deliberate exclusion decision and confirm with the relevant Account Manager. Target: 100% ERP/CRM account count alignment for active accounts.
  • H1 · Month 1–2Resolve the 6 Sanofi-migrated duplicate records: confirm the ERP record as the single authoritative master; archive or correct the CRM record to mirror ERP; document the resolution in both systems. These six accounts overlap with the four PP-01 discrepant accounts — the credit limit reconciliation under R-01 covers this simultaneously.
  • H1 · Month 2–3Publish the CRM update governance protocol (aligned with the approval workflow implemented under R-01): all credit limit and payment terms field updates in CRM require Credit Controller written approval before execution; account setup in CRM for any new account must reference the ERP customer number; CS Team Lead to review CRM account completeness quarterly against ERP as part of the existing O2C self-assessment.

§3   Implementation Sequencing
3.1   Three Horizons Framework

The five recommendations are sequenced across three implementation horizons aligned to the locked O2C Strategy Profile. H1 (0–6 months) targets all immediate governance closures and quick wins — the items that reduce active financial and compliance risk fastest. H2 (6–12 months) delivers the automation layer that sustains H1 governance gains at scale. H3 (12–18+ months) completes the structural infrastructure migration that eliminates the TSA dependency.

Wave 1 (H1 — Weeks 1–6): R-02 (EDI SOP/RACI, fastest path to governance), parallel launch of R-01 immediate credit reconciliation (top 4 accounts), and R-03 programme initiation (TSA Annex C, lead assignment).
Wave 2 (H1 — Months 2–6): R-01 extended reconciliation to top 50 accounts; R-04 EDI monitoring configuration and seasonal protocol; R-05 customer master deduplication; R-03 Carrefour France migration execution.
Wave 3 (H2–H3 — Months 6–18+): R-01 and R-04 automation pipelines; R-03 remaining TSA connection migrations (Boots UK legacy, dm, E.Leclerc, Lidl, Coop legacy).
