Section 1
Domain Overview
1.1   Context and Scope

The Opella Europe Order-to-Cash (O2C) process encompasses all commercial and operational activities from the receipt of a customer purchase order through to cash collection. The domain spans four functional teams — Customer Service, Finance and Credit Control, Distribution Centre Operations, and Accounts Receivable — and serves 340 active customer accounts across the Opella Europe commercial region covering France, the United Kingdom, Germany, Spain, Italy, Benelux, Austria, Switzerland, and wider EMEA markets.

This assessment covers the O2C domain as operated under Opella's full operational independence from Sanofi, effective April 2025. All findings are derived from the 12-document discovery corpus and reflect the verified state of the domain as of Q4 2025 – January 2026.

Opella is a global Fast-Moving Consumer Healthcare (FMCH) company, formed through the carve-out of Sanofi's Consumer Healthcare division in 2024. The O2C process was originally designed to support a pharma-distributor commercial model. As of 2025, 67.3% of total order volume arrives via retail Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) — a structural shift from the model the process was originally built for, and the primary driver of complexity in the current state.

Note — EDI Fulfilment Seasonality

EDI fulfilment rates degrade materially during two recurring cluster periods: April–May and October–November. During these windows, the EDI fulfilment rate drops from the standard 91% to approximately 59%, generating elevated manual CS workload and SLA risk on major retail accounts. This pattern is cross-domain in origin — demand forecast misses in the upstream supply chain generate excess orders that arrive simultaneously via EDI, creating credit hold and exception queue congestion in the O2C process.

Section 3
Process Inventory

This section documents each process step in full, covering the responsible actors, systems involved, procedural steps, key decision points, and applicable timelines. All details are sourced from the formal Opella governance documents (OPS-EU-CS-SOP-001, OPS-EU-FIN-POL-002, OPS-EU-CS-RACI-001) and the informal working documentation for EDI (OPS-EU-IT-WN-001).

3.1   Order Receipt — EDI Channel

Responsible: Opella Digital (J. Okafor) for 8 Opella-managed connections; Sanofi Shared Services (via TSA) for 6 Sanofi-managed connections. CS Team Lead informed on exceptions.

Retail customers transmit purchase orders directly via EDI to Opella's EDI middleware. For the 8 Opella Digital-managed connections, orders are received, validated against Opella's product mapping tables, and posted automatically into SAP S/4HANA. An order acknowledgement is returned to the customer's system automatically on successful posting. For the 6 Sanofi-managed connections (Carrefour France, Boots UK, dm Drogerie Markt, E.Leclerc, Lidl Europe, Coop Group), orders are transmitted to and routed through Sanofi Shared Services' EDI infrastructure before reaching Opella's SAP S/4HANA system. Issues on these connections require contact with the Sanofi IT helpdesk (+33 1 53 77 40 00, option 3). Response time for diagnosis on Sanofi-managed connections is 24–48 hours for standard failures; same day for Priority 1 (complete connection outage).

EDI exception types and resolution procedures are documented in OPS-EU-IT-WN-001. Common exception types include: order not received (EDI transmission failure), duplicate orders, product mapping errors, and credit holds on arrival. Exceptions requiring manual intervention are escalated to the CS queue. 34 such manual EDI escalations were recorded in the 2025 Customer Service Escalation Log (Doc 06).

3.2   Order Receipt — Manual and Email Channels

Responsible: Customer Service Representative (CSR). Accountable: CS Team Lead (A. Kovacs).

Telephone orders are entered into SAP S/4HANA by the CSR within 30 minutes of the call. The CSR must verify the caller's authorisation status in SAP before accepting the order and must verbally confirm all order details (SKU, quantity, delivery address, PO reference) before ending the call. A written confirmation is issued within 4 business hours of SAP entry.

Email orders are acknowledged within 2 business hours of receipt using the standard template (Opella CS SharePoint). If any required information is missing (SKU, quantity, requested delivery date, delivery address, PO reference), the CSR contacts the customer before SAP entry. Entry must occur within 1 business day of email receipt, and the order confirmation is issued within 2 business hours of entry.

Order cut-off times for same-day processing apply by market: France 14:00 CET; UK 13:00 GMT; Germany/Austria/Switzerland 14:00 CET; Spain 14:00 CET; Benelux 14:00 CET; Italy 13:00 CET (via 3PL); Rest of Europe 12:00 CET (best efforts).

3.3   Order Validation

Responsible: CSR (manual and email channels); SAP S/4HANA automated checks (EDI channel). Accountable: CS Team Lead.

All orders — regardless of channel — must pass the following validation sequence in SAP S/4HANA before proceeding to credit assessment. The steps are mandated by OPS-EU-CS-SOP-001 §5 and must be completed in the order listed:

  • Customer account status check: Active → proceed; Credit Review → notify Credit Controller within 4 hours before proceeding; On Hold → notify CS Team Lead and Account Manager within 1 hour, do not process; Inactive/Closed → do not process under any circumstances.
  • Credit limit verification: SAP S/4HANA credit module checks cumulative outstanding balance against the approved credit limit. The SAP S/4HANA ERP figure is the sole authoritative basis for this check. Credit figures from SAP CRM or verbal guidance from Account Managers must not be used without Credit Controller written confirmation.
  • Product availability check: Confirmed against SAP S/4HANA inventory management. Partial availability triggers a customer offer of partial shipment with confirmed back-order date. Discontinued SKUs require CS Team Lead consultation before customer notification.
  • Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ) check: Verified against account-tier configuration in SAP. Sub-MOQ orders require Account Manager written approval within 2 business hours before SAP entry proceeds.
  • Pricing validation: Customer PO prices compared against the SAP S/4HANA price list for the account. Variances <2% processed at SAP price with Account Manager notification. Variances ≥2% require customer contact to resolve before processing. Structural pricing errors (wrong price list applied) are escalated to CS Team Lead immediately; order is held.

3.4   Credit Assessment

Responsible: SAP S/4HANA credit module (automatic hold trigger); Raj Patel, Credit Controller, UK & Europe (hold review and release decision). Accountable: Marc Dupont, Finance Director, Europe.

Credit assessment is performed automatically by SAP S/4HANA at order entry. Orders whose processing would cause the customer's cumulative outstanding balance to exceed the approved credit limit are placed on automatic credit hold. The SAP system also applies automatic holds where an invoice is more than 60 days overdue. The Credit Controller may additionally apply manual credit holds at any time without an automatic trigger.

On credit hold, the CSR must notify the Credit Controller within 4 business hours, providing: customer name and SAP account number, order reference and total value, current outstanding balance, and approved credit limit. The Credit Controller's options are: (a) release with documented commercial justification; (b) approve a temporary limit increase (maximum 90 days, expiry date mandatory); (c) request partial payment as a condition of release; (d) reject the order. All decisions must be in writing before hold release. A CSR who releases a credit hold without written Credit Controller authorisation constitutes a material policy breach, escalated to the Finance Director.

Credit limit approval authority by band (per Credit Management Policy Appendix A): up to EUR 250,000 — Credit Controller; EUR 250,001–500,000 — Finance Director; EUR 500,001–1,000,000 — Finance Director and VP Commercial Operations; above EUR 1,000,000 — Finance Director, VP Commercial Operations, and CEO.

3.5   Order Confirmation

Responsible: CSR. Accountable: CS Team Lead (A. Kovacs).

Once all validation steps are satisfied, the CSR issues a formal written order confirmation. For telephone orders, confirmation is by email to the customer's designated contact within 4 business hours of SAP entry. For email orders, confirmation is by reply to the original order email within 2 business hours of SAP entry. No order may be released to the Distribution Centre before the confirmation has been issued.

Mandatory confirmation content: Opella SAP sales order number; customer PO reference; confirmed SKUs, descriptions, and quantities; confirmed unit prices and total order value (ex-VAT); applicable VAT; confirmed delivery address; confirmed despatch and expected delivery date by market; CSR name and direct contact details.

3.6   Order Fulfilment and Despatch

Responsible: Distribution Centre Operations. Accountable: CS Team Lead (A. Kovacs, for order monitoring and exception escalation).

Following order confirmation, the CSR releases the order to the allocated Distribution Centre via SAP S/4HANA. DC allocation is determined automatically by SAP based on the customer's account record and product inventory position. Non-standard DC allocations require CS Team Lead confirmation before release.

3.7   Invoicing

Responsible: SAP S/4HANA (automated). Accountable: Marc Dupont, Finance Director, Europe.

Invoices are generated automatically in SAP S/4HANA upon the DC's goods issue posting. The invoice reflects the confirmed order value and is transmitted to the customer on the day of despatch. Standard payment terms by account tier: Tier 1 (Major Retail — annual Opella purchases above EUR 2M) NET45 from invoice date, with 1% early payment discount for settlement within 15 days; Tier 2 (Pharmacy and Wholesale) NET30; Tier 3 (Independent) NET30; New accounts (first 6 months) NET14 or cash-with-order per Credit Controller instruction. All deviations from standard terms require written Finance Director approval and must be documented in the SAP customer record.

3.8   Accounts Receivable Management

Responsible: Raj Patel, Credit Controller, UK & Europe. Accountable: Marc Dupont, Finance Director, Europe. Informed: Sophie Marchetti, VP Commercial Operations.

Invoice aging is tracked continuously in the SAP AR module. The Credit Controller produces a monthly aged debtors report, distributed to the Finance Director and VP Commercial Operations by the 5th working day of each month. Accounts more than 60 days overdue are individually reviewed in the monthly Finance and Commercial review meeting.

The quarterly self-assessment cycle requires the Credit Controller to review credit limit data accuracy in SAP S/4HANA against approved credit files, identify ERP/CRM data discrepancies, and review holds pending resolution. This self-assessment is a compliance requirement under OPS-EU-FIN-POL-002 §8.2.

4.1   Organisational Structure

The O2C function at Opella Europe operates across two organisational lines: Commercial Operations (Customer Service, Account Management) and Finance (Credit Control, AR, Finance Director). The VP Commercial Operations holds functional accountability for the end-to-end O2C process, with Finance Director accountability for all credit and AR decisions.

The Customer Service function comprises 4 FTE (CS Team Lead plus 3 CSRs) as of June 2025. EDI technical operations sit within Opella Digital, a separate function, with a formal interface to CS for exception escalation.

5.2   Integration Summary

The integration topology for the Opella Europe O2C domain is hub-and-spoke, with SAP S/4HANA as the central hub. All order intake channels (EDI Middleware and manual entry) post into S/4HANA. All downstream fulfilment, financial, and AR activities originate from S/4HANA. The one external dependency outside this hub — the Sanofi EDI Infrastructure — is not under Opella's direct control and introduces a break in the otherwise Opella-governed integration chain.

Integration Dependency Note — Sanofi EDI Infrastructure (I-02)

Integration point I-02 represents the single externally-controlled dependency in the Opella Europe O2C integration topology. The 6 Sanofi-managed EDI connections (Carrefour France, Boots UK, dm Drogerie Markt, E.Leclerc, Lidl Europe, Coop Group) collectively account for an estimated portion of the 5,667 annual EDI orders and cover some of Opella's highest-value retail accounts. Opella Digital does not have access to Sanofi's EDI infrastructure and cannot independently resolve outages or mapping errors on these connections. Resolution requires engagement with the Sanofi IT helpdesk under the Transition Services Agreement, with response times of 24–48 hours for standard issues. Transfer of these connections to Opella Digital control is on the TSA review list; no confirmed transfer date is documented in the discovery corpus.
