1.1   Discovery Context

AuroPro's autonomous discovery analysis of 12 input documents — spanning process SOPs, credit policy, RACI documentation, EDI integration registers, AR review notes, and four structured data exports totalling 8,420 order records and 340 customer master entries — has identified five structured pain points within Opella Europe's Order-to-Cash domain.

All five findings share a common structural origin: the April 2025 legal and operational separation from Sanofi was executed on a timeline that prioritised legal independence over data governance and process completeness. Three patterns recur across the findings: data inheritance without a defined system-of-record protocol; process documentation adapted from Sanofi predecessors with material sections removed; and operational dependencies on Sanofi infrastructure that remain unresolved fourteen months after carve-out. These patterns constitute what this report terms carve-out inheritance debt — latent risks embedded in the operating model that will compound unless addressed as part of a coordinated programme.

Credit Data Integrity Risk
High Severity Blocking — Discovery Copilot Data Governance

Four of Opella Europe's highest-revenue trading accounts carry irreconcilable credit limit discrepancies between SAP S/4HANA — the sole authoritative system of record per Credit Management Policy OPS-EU-FIN-POL-002, §4.1 — and SAP CRM, where limits and payment terms were manually updated by account managers following the April 2025 carve-out without documented approval. The Credit Controller (Raj Patel) escalated this formally in his Q4 2025 Accounts Receivable Review, noting that for Carrefour France, the account is "currently operating against the CRM limit" and that "our credit policy does not define which system is authoritative."

Business Impact

The aggregate CRM overstatement of €1,450,000 represents credit extended beyond Opella's policy-approved limits for four of its largest retail partners. For Boots UK and Carrefour France, the concurrent payment terms conflict (CRM: NET30 vs. ERP: NET45) creates an additional risk: commercial teams may be invoicing on terms that do not match the contractual standard, potentially generating invoice disputes and accelerating cash flow obligations that the credit policy does not sanction. The Credit Management Policy (§4.2) requires written Credit Controller approval before any limit change takes effect — this approval is absent for all four accounts.

Policy Breach — OPS-EU-FIN-POL-002
Section 4.2 of the Credit Management Policy explicitly states that "only the Credit Controller has authority to create or modify customer credit limits in SAP S/4HANA." CRM updates made by account managers post-carve-out, without Credit Controller documented approval, constitute a material policy breach that must be remediated before Q1 2026 close.

Operational Consequence

With no documented accountability, any EDI incident — connection failure, mapping error, duplicate order, or credit-hold interaction — is handled ad hoc. The escalation log records 34 EDI-related customer service cases in 2025, each requiring manual intervention. Resolution times range from 4 hours (Opella Digital-managed connections) to 48 hours (Sanofi TSA-managed connections, compounded by the absence of a clear escalation protocol). The Sanofi predecessor SOP (SCH-OPS-CS-SOP-001 v4.0) covered EDI with a full dedicated section; Opella's adaptation removed it entirely, creating an immediate governance gap at the date of carve-out.

Risk Amplifier
Because the EDI SOP and RACI were explicitly deferred to Q3 2025 and remain unpublished as of November 2025, any audit of the O2C process would identify that Opella's primary order channel — representing 5,667 orders and the majority of annual revenue — operates without formal accountability documentation. This creates audit, compliance, and business continuity risk simultaneously.

Medium Severity Amber — Carry Forward Operational Resilience

Order flow analysis across 8,420 orders reveals systematic EDI fulfilment rate degradation during two recurring calendar windows — April–May and October–November 2025 — from a baseline of approximately 91% to approximately 59%. During these same periods, the customer service escalation log records 19 stockout-related customer complaints from Carrefour France, E.Leclerc, Boots UK, and Tesco UK, with notes including "Customer threatened to delist Doliprane if EDI reliability does not improve." The co-occurrence of EDI channel degradation and stockout complaints across both periods is consistent with a cross-domain supply-demand alignment failure amplified by EDI instability, rather than a pure channel issue.

§3   Root Cause Analysis
3.1   Structural Origin — Carve-Out Inheritance Debt

All five pain points share a common structural origin: the April 2025 legal separation from Sanofi was completed on a schedule that prioritised legal independence over operational governance completeness. Three recurring patterns emerge across the evidence:

Pattern A — Data inheritance without governance: Credit limits, customer records, and payment terms were migrated from Sanofi systems into both SAP ERP and SAP CRM simultaneously, without a defined system-of-record decision. Both datasets were live at carve-out and neither was formally reconciled. Account managers subsequently updated CRM without triggering ERP alignment, creating the discrepancies documented in PP-01 and PP-05.

Pattern B — Process documentation adapted with sections removed: The Opella Order Management SOP was derived directly from the Sanofi Consumer Healthcare SOP v4.0 (Jan 2023). The Sanofi predecessor contained a full EDI section (§11). This section was removed in the Opella adaptation, with a commitment to produce a standalone EDI SOP by Q3 2025. That commitment was not met (PP-02).

Pattern C — Operational infrastructure dependency unresolved: Six EDI connections that could not be technically transferred at carve-out were placed under TSA governance as an interim arrangement. Fourteen months later, four of six have no migration lead assigned and no scoping underway, with target dates extending to Q3 2026 (PP-03, PP-04).

§4   Opportunity Inventory

Each pain point maps to a primary remediation opportunity. OPP
01
Automated Credit Limit Reconciliation
Addresses: PP-01, PP-05  |  Type: AI + Data  |  AI Potential: High
Design and deploy an automated reconciliation pipeline that extracts credit limit and payment terms data from both SAP S/4HANA (authoritative) and SAP CRM on a scheduled basis, identifies discrepancies beyond a defined materiality threshold, and routes divergences to the Credit Controller for approval or CRM correction. AI can assist in identifying the "true" intended limit from audit trail patterns, correspondence, and approval history. The pipeline also enforces the policy principle that only the Credit Controller can authorise CRM updates, by flagging unauthorised manual changes in real time.
Impact: 5/5 Effort: 3/5 AI Potential: High AI Opportunity
OPP
02
EDI SOP & RACI Authoring
Addresses: PP-02  |  Type: Process  |  AI Potential: Partial
Author the formal EDI Order Management SOP and RACI addendum that were committed in Q3 2025 and remain unpublished. The Sanofi Consumer Healthcare O2C SOP v4.0 (SCH-OPS-CS-SOP-001, §11) and the existing CS Working Notes (OPS-EU-IT-WN-001) provide the procedural basis. AI can accelerate drafting by synthesising the working notes, Sanofi SOP section, and EDI Integration Register into a structured draft that the CS Team Lead reviews and approves. This is the highest-impact, lowest-effort opportunity: it closes a governance gap affecting two-thirds of order volume with minimal technical investment.
Impact: 4/5 Effort: 1/5 AI Potential: Partial Process Quick Win
OPP
03
TSA Connection Migration Programme
Addresses: PP-03, PP-04  |  Type: Strategic Programme  |  AI Potential: Low
Establish a formal programme to accelerate migration of the 6 Sanofi-managed EDI connections to Opella Digital management, aligned with the IBM Sterling B2B Integrator platform. Priority order: Connection #9 (Carrefour France — Q4 2025 target already missed), then #10/#11 (Boots UK legacy and dm — Q1 2026 targets at risk). Assign named migration leads for connections 12–14. Secure TSA Schedule Annex C from the Opella TSA Programme Office and incorporate its SLA terms into the operational risk register. This is the highest-effort opportunity but directly addresses the most commercially consequential external dependency.
Impact: 4/5 Effort: 5/5 AI Potential: Low Strategic Programme
OPP
04
EDI Monitoring & Anomaly Detection
Addresses: PP-04  |  Type: AI/Automation  |  AI Potential: High
Deploy real-time EDI channel monitoring with AI-powered anomaly detection to identify connection failures, mapping errors, duplicate orders, and fulfilment rate degradation before they generate customer escalations. The system should ingest the EDI event stream from Opella Digital's IBM Sterling platform and alert the CS Team Lead within minutes of an anomaly. Pattern detection across seasonal demand windows (April–May, October–November) can trigger proactive manual order coverage protocols before fulfilment rates decline. This directly addresses the 34-incident escalation pattern and the stockout correlation identified in PP-04.
Impact: 3/5 Effort: 2/5 AI Potential: High AI Opportunity
OPP
05
Customer Master Deduplication & Governance
Addresses: PP-05  |  Type: Data  |  AI Potential: Partial
Conduct a full reconciliation of the 22 accounts present in SAP S/4HANA but absent from SAP CRM, and resolve the 6 duplicate records for Sanofi-migrated accounts. Establish a governance protocol (approved workflow + approval record in SAP) for any future CRM credit limit update, aligned with the requirements of Credit Management Policy §4.2. AI can assist in record matching across systems and in identifying which record variant represents the most recently verified data. Target: top 50 accounts reconciled before end of Q1 2026, as recommended by Raj Patel in the AR Review Notes.
Impact: 3/5 Effort: 3/5 AI Potential: Partial Data Initiative
