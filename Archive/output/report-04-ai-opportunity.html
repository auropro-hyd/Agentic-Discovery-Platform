Reading This Report
OPP-01 and OPP-04 were identified and scoped in Report 02 as AI-assisted opportunities. This report provides the full specification required to commission, evaluate, and accept each use case. The audience is twofold: (1) business owners who must validate the problem framing and output design, and (2) technical leads who will build or procure the implementation. Sections 2 and 3 are written to serve both audiences — the "AI Reasoning Approach" subsections explain what the AI does in plain language; the "Output Design & Integration" subsections contain the technical interface contract.
Human-in-the-Loop Requirement (H1 Non-Negotiable)

This constraint is deliberate: governance confidence must be established before any automated write-back is considered.

§2 AI Use Case 01 — Credit Limit Reconciliation Engine
OPP-01
Credit Limit Reconciliation Engine
Automated weekly reconciliation of ERP vs CRM credit data — discrepancy classification, policy breach detection, resolution recommendation
Critical Priority H1 Deployment AI-Assisted
2.1 Problem Statement
The SAP S/4HANA ERP and SAP CRM systems hold inconsistent credit limit records for 8 of Opella's highest-value accounts. The Credit Management Policy (OPS-EU-FIN-POL-002 §4.1) is unambiguous: ERP is the sole system of record. CRM figures are not authoritative. However, CRM is the system Account Managers access daily — and there is no automated mechanism to detect when these systems diverge, nor to flag when credit decisions are being made against the lower-authority figure.
The current state produces two compounding risks: (1) EDI orders for accounts over their ERP limit may be auto-released because CS is checking CRM, and (2) the Credit Controller cannot identify which accounts require reconciliation without manual comparison of two system exports.

§2 AI Use Case 01 — Credit Limit Reconciliation Engine (continued)
2.2 AI Reasoning Approach
The engine is a large language model (LLM) reasoning pipeline that ingests the joined ERP–CRM dataset and the credit policy, and produces a structured reconciliation report. The reasoning chain has four steps:
StepActionAI Task TypeOutput
1. Join & Diff Match ERP and CRM records on customer_id; identify accounts present in ERP but missing from CRM (22 accounts); compute delta per matched record Deterministic (script) Discrepancy table JSON
2. Classify For each discrepancy, classify cause: (A) Sanofi migration artifact — migration_source = "Sanofi Legacy System" and delta > 0; (B) AM override — last_updated_by is an account manager name; (C) System drift — delta present with no identifiable cause LLM classification Cause tag per record
3. Policy Check For each discrepancy, check against Policy §4.1 (ERP governs), §4.2 (only Credit Controller may modify), §4.4 (review triggers): flag as Breach / Review Required / Informational LLM rule application Policy status per record
4. Recommend Generate a 1–2 sentence recommended action per account: update CRM to match ERP, escalate to Credit Controller, or schedule formal review — grounded in Policy §4.3 approval tiers LLM generation Action text per record
2.3 Output Design
The engine produces a structured Reconciliation Report delivered as a formatted document to the Credit Controller (Raj Patel) each Monday morning. The report has three sections:
Section A — Breach Summary
  • All accounts where CRM limit exceeds ERP limit and a credit decision may have been made against the CRM figure
  • Each row: account, delta, cause classification, breach severity, recommended action
  • Sorted by overstatement amount descending
  • Policy section reference on each row
Section B — Missing from CRM
  • 22 accounts present in ERP but absent from CRM — cannot be credit-checked by AM without ERP access
  • Flag: "AM cannot verify credit limit — recommend CRM backfill"
  • Owner: Raj Patel to confirm or reassign

§3 AI Use Case 02 — EDI Monitoring & Anomaly Detection
OPP-04
EDI Monitoring & Anomaly Detection
Real-time detection of EDI channel failures, duplicate orders, mapping errors, and fulfilment rate degradation — with TSA-aware alert routing
High Priority H1 Deployment AI-Assisted
3.1 Problem Statement
EDI accounts for 67.3% of Opella Europe's order volume (5,667 of 8,420 orders in the 2025 dataset). Fulfilment rate on the EDI channel drops from 91% baseline to 59% during seasonal cluster periods (April–May and October–November) — a 32-percentage-point degradation that maps directly to the 19 stockout complaints recorded in the CS escalation log. The current detection mechanism is: a customer calls to complain.
The root cause is a monitoring gap, not a technical failure. The 34 EDI incidents recorded in 2025 were all discovered reactively. There is no automated check that compares expected order acknowledgements against received ones, no alert when a connection's fulfilment rate begins to degrade, and no system that distinguishes between the 8 Opella Digital-managed connections (James Okafor, same-day resolution) and the 6 Sanofi-managed connections (Sanofi IT helpdesk, 24–48hr resolution) for routing purposes.
Data Inputs for Detection Engine
Real-time / Near-real-time
  • EDI message acknowledgement log (CONTRL / APERAK messages from trading partners)
  • Order management system event stream — order created, order on hold, order released events
  • CS escalation log feed — new case creation triggers cross-reference check
Reference / Batch
  • Connection registry — 14 connections, 8 Opella-managed vs 6 Sanofi-managed (Doc 05 Annex)
  • Order flow baseline CSV (Doc 10) — 91% EDI fulfilment rate as control
  • Seasonal calendar — Apr–May and Oct–Nov flagged as high-risk windows
  • RACI Matrix — owner lookup for alert routing
3.2 Detection Pattern Library
The engine monitors for the following pattern types, derived from the 34 EDI incidents in the CS escalation log and the CS working notes authored by Alina Kovacs (Doc 05):
P-01
Acknowledgement Timeout
No CONTRL acknowledgement received within 30 minutes of ORDERS message transmission. Trigger: single occurrence for Priority-1 accounts (Carrefour FR, Boots UK); 3 occurrences within 24h for all others. Evidence base: "EDI acknowledgement not received from partner. Manual re-entry required." (34 EDI case notes).
P-02
Duplicate Order Detection
Two orders from the same customer on the same date with identical PO reference number detected within the order management event stream. Trigger: immediate on any occurrence. Evidence base: "Order duplicate detected on EDI channel. Customer notified." (CS log). Resolution: cancel duplicate, issue email confirmation — do not use EDI for cancellation.
P-03
Mapping Error Cluster
Three or more orders from the same connection rejected within 48 hours due to unrecognised product codes. Indicates a catalogue update on the customer's side that was not communicated to Opella. Evidence base: "EDI mapping error — incorrect product code in ORDERS message." Escalation differs by connection owner (see §3.3).
P-04
Fulfilment Rate Degradation
Rolling 7-day EDI fulfilment rate for any single connection falls below 80% (threshold: 91% baseline minus 11pp buffer). During seasonal window (Apr–May, Oct–Nov), threshold tightens to 85%. Trigger: daily batch calculation. Evidence base: 59% cluster-period fulfilment rate detected in order flow analysis (Doc 10).
P-05
Credit Hold on EDI Order
EDI order automatically placed on credit hold because order value would push account over credit limit. Cross-reference: if account has an ERP–CRM discrepancy (OPP-01 output), flag for Credit Controller before release — do not release on CRM limit alone. Evidence base: "check with Raj before releasing… ERP limit is lower" (Doc 05 §4).

The 6 Sanofi-managed connections have a fundamentally different resolution path and SLA. Routing an Opella Digital ticket for a Sanofi-managed connection (or vice versa) costs hours. The alert system must encode this distinction explicitly.

3.4 Seasonal Window Protocol
The order flow analysis (Doc 10) confirms two seasonal degradation clusters: April–May (cold/allergy season, Allegra/Doliprane demand spike) and October–November (winter season, Doliprane 500mg stockouts confirmed in CS log). During these windows, the monitoring parameters tighten and the CS team capacity allocation increases.
Seasonal Window Adjustments
  • Fulfilment rate alert threshold: 85% (vs 80% off-season)
  • P-01 acknowledgement timeout: 15 min (vs 30 min off-season)
  • CS duty cover: minimum 2 agents trained on EDI procedures
  • Pre-window check: run P-03 mapping check 2 weeks before window open — catalogue changes cluster pre-season
Success Criteria
  • EDI fulfilment rate ≥91% year-round (including seasonal windows)
  • Mean time to detect EDI failure: <15 min (from 24–48h manual detection)
  • Zero Sanofi-managed connection escalations resolved by wrong team
  • 19 stockout complaint equivalents in 2025 → target <5 in first seasonal window post-deployment
  • All P1 alerts acknowledged within 4 hours, 100% of the time

§6 AI Governance & Risk
AI systems in financial and operational workflows require explicit governance: who validates outputs, what happens when the AI is wrong, and how confidence degrades over time. The framework below is proportionate to the H1 scope — lightweight, human-in-the-loop, and designed to build institutional confidence before any H2 automation is considered.
Oversight Model
OPP-01 — Human Validation Gate
  • Every weekly report reviewed by Credit Controller (Raj Patel) before any SAP action is taken
  • Credit Controller records action taken against each flagged account in a simple log (Excel or SharePoint)
  • Finance Director (Marc Dupont) receives copy of report; reviews summary metrics monthly
  • No ERP write-back without Credit Controller signature in H1
OPP-04 — Human Validation Gate
  • All alerts require CS team acknowledgement before action is taken — no auto-routing to Sanofi helpdesk
  • CS agent confirms or dismisses each alert within 2 hours; dismissal requires free-text reason
  • James Okafor reviews alert log weekly; approves or adjusts detection thresholds
  • Alert false-positive rate tracked weekly; threshold adjustment if >20% false-positive rate

H2 Automation Gating Criteria

Automated write-back (OPP-01 updating SAP CRM directly) or automated alert dispatch (OPP-04 calling Sanofi helpdesk without CS review) will only be considered if: (1) H1 accuracy rate ≥95% over 3 consecutive months; (2) Credit Controller and CS team lead formally sign off on the specific automation; (3) legal and compliance review is completed for FMCH regulatory requirements. No H2 automation is in scope for this report.
