2Root cause

How the issues found trace back to shared structural causes:

How the issues trace back to shared causes
Left: structural causes. Right: the pain points each one drives.

Pattern A — Data inheritance without governance. Credit limits, customer records, and payment terms were migrated from Sanofi systems into both SAP ERP and SAP CRM simultaneously, without a defined system-of-record decision. Account managers subsequently updated CRM without triggering ERP alignment, creating the discrepancies in PP-01 and PP-05.

Pattern B — Process documentation adapted with sections removed. The Opella Order Management SOP was derived from the Sanofi Consumer Healthcare SOP v4.0 (Jan 2023). The Sanofi predecessor contained a full EDI section (§11). This section was removed in the Opella adaptation, with a commitment to produce a standalone EDI SOP by Q3 2025. That commitment was not met (PP-02).

Pattern C — Operational infrastructure dependency unresolved. Six EDI connections that could not be technically transferred at carve-out were placed under TSA governance as an interim arrangement. Fourteen months later, four of six have no migration lead assigned and no scoping underway, with target dates extending to Q3 2026 (PP-03, PP-04).

PP
01
Credit Data Integrity Risk
High SeverityData Governance / Credit

Four accounts carry CRM credit limits above the ERP-authoritative value, totalling €1,450,000 in CRM overstatement. The Credit Management Policy (OPS-EU-FIN-POL-002) designates SAP S/4HANA as the sole authoritative source for all customer credit limits, yet post-carve-out CRM edits were not reflected in ERP. Carrefour France carries the largest single discrepancy at €600,000 (CRM €2.4M vs ERP €1.8M).

PP
03
TSA-Managed Connection Dependency
High SeverityThird-Party Risk / EDI

Six of Opella's 14 EDI connections remain under Sanofi Shared Services (TSA) governance as an interim post-carve-out arrangement. These connections serve the domain's highest-revenue retail accounts — including Carrefour France, E.Leclerc, and Boots UK — and carry a 24–48 hour incident resolution SLA that Opella cannot control. The Carrefour France connection had a Q4 2025 migration target; it is already overdue. Four of six connections have no migration lead assigned and no scoping underway.

PP
04
EDI Channel Instability
Medium SeverityOperational Resilience / EDI

Order flow analysis identifies a systematic EDI fulfilment rate degradation to approximately 59% during two recurring cluster periods — April–May and October–November — against a standard baseline of 91%. The same two windows generated 19 stockout customer complaints in 2025 and represent the highest-volume EDI incident periods in the escalation log. The pattern is cross-domain in origin: upstream demand forecast misses generate excess orders arriving simultaneously via EDI, creating credit hold and exception queue congestion in the O2C process.

PP
05
Onboarding Data Integrity Risk
Medium SeverityData Governance / Customer Master

SAP S/4HANA contains 340 active customer records; SAP CRM contains 318 — a difference of 22 accounts that exist in the ERP but are not reflected in CRM. Six accounts migrated from the Sanofi legacy customer master in May 2024 exist as active records in both systems, each carrying different credit limits and payment terms. The Retail Customer Onboarding Guide instructs the CS Team Lead to "create the customer record in all systems" without naming which systems are authoritative or defining a hierarchy when records diverge.
