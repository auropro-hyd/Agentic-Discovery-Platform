Current State Assessment

How Order-to-Cash runs today. A factual baseline of the process, the systems that support it, and how information is structured — stated as fact, no judgements.

1Domain overview

1.1Context and scope

This section documents the factual current state of the organisation Europe's order-to-cash (O2C) domain following the carve-out from Sanofi. The domain spans retail customer order capture across multiple channels (EDI, manual telephone, email and fax), order fulfilment, customer service escalation handling, and credit management governed by customer master data held in two systems — SAP CRM and SAP S/4HANA. EDI is the dominant order channel by both volume and value, while the published Order Management SOP and the O2C RACI cover only the manual telephone and email channels. Customer credit limits and payment terms are maintained in parallel across CRM and the ERP, with SAP S/4HANA designated as the sole authoritative source.

Retail orders enter the organisation through four channels. EDI is the largest, carrying 5,667 orders (67.3% of volume) worth EUR 59,711,399.53 (66.8% of value). Manual telephone and email orders are processed under the published Order Management SOP and the O2C RACI; both documents explicitly exclude the EDI channel. Fax carries 184 orders worth EUR 1,771,828.67, accepted under derogation for legacy accounts. Orders flow to fulfilment. EDI fulfilment rates degrade from a ~91% baseline to approximately 59% during the April–May and October–November peak demand windows. Exceptions surface through a customer-service escalation process; 'EDI order not processed — manual intervention required' accounts for 34 escalations (43% share). Credit governance draws on customer master data maintained in SAP CRM and SAP S/4HANA. SAP S/4HANA holds 340 active customer records; SAP CRM holds 318. SAP S/4HANA is designated the sole authoritative source for customer credit limits per Credit Management Policy OPS-EU-FIN-POL-002.

3Process inventory

Each stage of the process in detail — how it runs today, who performs it, and on which system.

EDI order capture

Retail customer / EDI connection · EDI

Electronic orders enter via EDI, which accounts for around 67% of total order volume — 5,667 orders (67.3%) worth EUR 59,711,399.53 (66.8% of value). The published Order Management SOP states EDI is not covered by that version of the SOP, and the O2C RACI excludes EDI-related rows. Working notes for EDI dispute resolution are maintained outside the official SOP set.

Source: EDI Dispute Resolution CS Working Notes, Order Management SOP Europe and O2C Process RACI Europe

Manual and email order capture

Customer Service order handler · SAP S/4HANA

Telephone and email orders are processed under the Order Management SOP, which defines the manual (telephone) and email order channels in Section 4. The O2C RACI covers the manual (telephone) and email order channels only.

Source: Order Management SOP Europe and O2C Process RACI Europe

Fax order capture

Customer Service order handler · Fax / manual entry

A very small number of fax orders from legacy accounts in certain markets are accepted under derogation. The fax channel carries 184 orders worth EUR 1,771,828.67, of which 40 close as not fulfilled.

Source: Sanofi Consumer Healthcare O2C SOP 2023 and Order Flow Analysis Export 2025

Escalation handling

Customer Service · Customer-service escalation log

Order and delivery exceptions are logged and resolved through customer service. 'EDI order not processed — manual intervention required' accounts for 34 escalations (43% share). Logged root causes also include delivery short / quantity variance, pricing discrepancy on invoice, delivery date change requested, returns authorisation, payment application query and credit note dispute.

Source: Customer Service Escalation Log 2025, EDI Dispute Resolution CS Working Notes and Order Flow Analysis Export 2025

Credit and master-data maintenance

Accounts Receivable / Credit · SAP CRM and SAP S/4HANA

Credit limits and payment terms are held in both SAP CRM and SAP S/4HANA. SAP S/4HANA holds 340 active customer records; SAP CRM holds 318 (22 accounts in ERP not reflected in CRM). Four accounts carry CRM credit limits above the ERP-authoritative value, totalling €1,450,000 in total CRM overstatement. The largest single discrepancy is Carrefour France at €600,000 (CRM €2,400,000 vs ERP €1,800,000). The Credit Management Policy (OPS-EU-FIN-POL-002) designates SAP S/4HANA as the sole authoritative source for all customer credit limits.

Source: Accounts Receivable Review Notes Q4 2025, Credit Management Policy Europe, SAP CRM Customer Export and SAP S/4HANA Customer Master Export

Each system that supports this process — what it is, how it is used, who owns it, and the constraints observed.

ERP and authoritative customer master

How it's used. Holds customer master, credit limits and payment terms; designated the sole authoritative source for all customer credit limits per OPS-EU-FIN-POL-002. Holds 340 active customer records (Dec 2025). 14 EDI connections active: 8 Opella Digital-managed, 6 under Sanofi TSA governance.

Ownership & access. Credit Management / Accounts Receivable

Observed constraints. Stated facts only.

SAP CRM

Customer relationship management system

How it's used. Maintains customer credit limits and payment terms in parallel with S/4HANA. CRM holds 318 active accounts (22 fewer than ERP). Four accounts carry CRM credit limits above the ERP-authoritative value; Carrefour France is the largest discrepancy at €600,000.

Ownership & access. Account management / Credit

Observed constraints. Carrefour France credit limit recorded as manually updated by account manager post-carve-out.
