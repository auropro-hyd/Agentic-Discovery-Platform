1.1Situation & opportunity

The situation

Opella Europe's order-to-cash operation inherits five structural gaps from the April 2025 carve-out from Sanofi. EDI carries 5,667 orders (67.3% of volume) yet is explicitly excluded from the Order Management SOP and the O2C RACI — leaving the dominant channel with no governed procedure, named owner, or dispute path. Four accounts carry CRM credit limits above the ERP-authoritative value, totalling €1,450,000 in overstatement; Carrefour France alone diverges by €600,000. Six of 14 EDI connections remain under Sanofi TSA governance with a 24–48 hour SLA Opella cannot control — four have no migration plan and the Carrefour France connection is already past its Q4 2025 target date. EDI fulfilment degrades to 59% during the April–May and October–November peak windows, generating 19 stockout complaints. SAP S/4HANA holds 340 customer accounts; SAP CRM holds 318 — 22 accounts are invisible to CRM-based credit and sales processes, and 6 Sanofi-migrated records carry inconsistent data across both systems.

The opportunity

All five pain points trace to three carve-out inheritance patterns rather than to operational failures — which means they are fixable with governance and tooling rather than structural redesign. The programme starts in H1 with the highest-leverage quick win: author the EDI SOP and RACI addendum (6 weeks, no IT dependency), deploy the automated credit reconciliation engine, and stand up EDI anomaly detection for the April–May cluster window. R-03 (TSA migration) begins in parallel and runs to H3. OPP-01 (AI credit engine) and OPP-04 (AI EDI monitoring) are both H1-deployable on existing data assets — no new infrastructure required. The full five-opportunity programme converges on a single operating model: SAP S/4HANA as the undisputed system of record, EDI fully governed, and both AI tools running in human-supervised mode.
