Discovery Copilot — Gap Resolution Audit Trail

Every open question the platform raised at the Block 2 gap gate, the source evidence that surfaced it, the resolutions the Copilot proposed, and the decision the AuroPro SME recorded — the complete chain of judgement that took the Order-to-Cash knowledge graph from raw findings to the clean, verified state the six client reports are generated from.

Why this artefact exists
The six client reports are deliberately clean — no conflict flags, no red/amber status, no "gap identified" language. That cleanliness is earned here. This working record is the audit behind it: it shows that nothing was hidden, only resolved. It is an internal SME artefact and is not shared with the client.

1How the gap gate works

Block 2 produces findings faster than any human could review them. The gap gate sorts those findings into three lanes and routes only what genuinely needs a human decision to the Discovery Copilot — the single human-in-the-loop surface, operated by the AuroPro SME, never the client.

3High-severity gaps — resolved before generation

Each of these blocked Block 3. For each, the Copilot proposed candidate resolutions grounded in the ingested evidence; the SME selected one (the chosen option is outlined in green) and the knowledge graph was retiered before the pipeline continued.

G1
Which system is authoritative for customer credit limits?
Contradiction · master dataHigh SeverityResolved
Open question about
Customer credit-limit system of record — SAP CRM vs SAP S/4HANA
Why it blocked
Credit decisions, order release and AR collections all read a credit limit. With two systems disagreeing on 267 of 318 accounts, the knowledge graph could not assert a single authoritative value — every downstream credit finding was unsafe to generate.
"CRM and ERP disagree on credit limits for 267 of 318 accounts, with an aggregate absolute delta of €30.68M (CRM €61.23M vs ERP €58.98M; largest single divergence Carrefour France €0.6M). Which value is authoritative, and are the CRM figures valid?"
Surfaced by: Accounts Receivable Review Notes Q4 2025 · SAP CRM Customer Export · SAP S/4HANA Customer Master Export

Candidate resolutions proposed by the Copilot

  • ATreat CRM as authoritative — it holds the most recently edited values, so the deltas may be deliberate updates.
  • BTreat S/4HANA as authoritative per the Credit Management Policy; classify the CRM deltas as post-carve-out drift to be reconciled. (SME selected)
  • CEscalate to the Finance Director for a manual ruling before proceeding.
SME
SME note · Option B selected
The credit policy names S/4HANA as the sole authoritative source, and the CRM export literally self-labels its biggest delta "manually updated by account manager post-carve-out." That's drift, not a real disagreement — so I'm tiering ERP verified and pushing the 267 mismatches into a reconciliation backlog rather than treating this as a contradiction to argue out.
S/4HANA → Verified tierSeeds OPP-01 — Credit Limit ReconciliationPrerequisite for OPP-03Unblocks Report 01 ownership map

Source documents: Credit Management Policy — Opella Europe, SAP S/4HANA Customer Master Export, SAP CRM Customer Export and Accounts Receivable Review Notes Q4 2025

G2
Who owns EDI order processing, and is there a controlled procedure?
Missing system-of-recordHigh SeverityResolved
Open question about
EDI order-processing ownership and the authoritative procedure governing it
Why it blocked
EDI carries 67.3% of order volume (5,667 orders, €59.71M) yet appears in no controlled document. Block 3 needs a process owner and procedure to map the dominant channel — without one, the knowledge graph could not tell whether this was a missing input (a document not yet ingested) or a genuine governance gap.
"EDI is the highest-volume channel but is excluded from the Order Management SOP, the O2C RACI, and the legacy Sanofi SOP — the only guidance is an unofficial customer-service working note. Is an authoritative EDI procedure simply un-ingested, or does none exist?"
Surfaced by: Order Management SOP · O2C Process RACI · EDI Dispute Resolution CS Working Notes · Order Flow Analysis Export 2025

Candidate resolutions proposed by the Copilot

  • AA controlled EDI SOP exists but was not ingested — request the document and re-run ingestion before proceeding.
  • BNo controlled procedure exists; the channel is governed only by an unofficial note — confirm as a genuine governance gap. (SME selected)
  • CEDI is owned end-to-end by the IT Integration Register, not Customer Service — reassign ownership there.
SME
SME note · Option B selected
The RACI itself is the tell — it says EDI rows were left out "pending formal documentation, target Q3 2025." So the procedure was never written; it isn't a file we failed to ingest. Okafor owns the connections technically, but nobody owns the order-handling process. Calling it a real governance gap and sending it straight to the opportunity set instead of waiting on a document that doesn't exist.
EDI process node → Amber (opportunity)Seeds OPP-01 / OPP-02Unblocks Report 01 process inventory

Source documents: O2C Process RACI — Opella Europe, Order Management SOP — Opella Europe, EDI Dispute Resolution — CS Working Notes, EDI Integration Register — Opella Europe and Order Flow Analysis Export 2025

G3
Does the credit-assessment control apply to the EDI channel?
Control coverage gapHigh SeverityResolved
Open question about
Whether the mandated credit check is enforced on retail EDI orders
Why it blocked
The Credit Management Policy mandates assessment, but order flow shows EDI orders reaching fulfilment with no credit gate. Block 3 had to know whether this was a policy exemption (no opportunity) or an unimplemented control (a material opportunity) before scoring credit risk.
"The Credit Management Policy requires a credit assessment, yet ~67% of EDI orders proceed to fulfilment without one. Is the EDI channel formally exempt, or is the control simply not implemented on the EDI order stream?"
Surfaced by: Credit Management Policy · Retail Customer Onboarding Guide · Order Flow Analysis Export 2025 · Sanofi Consumer Healthcare O2C SOP 2023

Candidate resolutions proposed by the Copilot

  • AThe EDI channel is formally exempt from credit assessment by policy design.
  • BThe policy applies universally, but no system-level credit gate exists on the EDI stream — confirmed coverage gap. (SME selected)
  • CManual credit review by the Credit team already covers EDI accounts adequately.
SME
SME note · Option B selected
The onboarding guide aligns credit assessment to the policy for every account — there's no EDI carve-out anywhere in the documents. The control was just never wired onto the retail EDI stream the business now runs on. So it's an unimplemented control over ~5,665 orders a year, not an exemption. This is the anchor for the credit-agent opportunity.
Coverage gap → Verified findingSeeds OPP-03 — TSA Connection MigrationUnblocks Report 03 sequencing

T1
Autonomy goal vs 6 EDI connections still managed by Sanofi
Strategic tension · TSA constraintClarificationAccepted
Open question about
Ownership of the 6 EDI connections under Sanofi Shared Services TSA
The tension
The stated direction is to consolidate and modernize toward autonomous operation, but the EDI Integration Register shows 6 of 14 connections (connections 9–14) remain under Sanofi Shared Services management — Opella cannot operate the dominant channel fully independently while they sit outside its control.
"8 connections under Opella Digital management, 6 under Sanofi Shared Services TSA … Connections 9–14 remain Sanofi Shared Services managed." For 6 EDI connections, exception recovery routes to the Sanofi IT helpdesk with a stated 24–48 hour turnaround.
Surfaced by: EDI Integration Register — Opella Europe · EDI Dispute Resolution CS Working Notes
SME
SME note · known and accepted
The TSA dependency is no surprise — it's written into the StrategyProfile as a constraint, so I'm not reopening the direction over it. The 6 connections become a planned ownership-transfer initiative in H1, which also clears the way for the exception pipeline to run autonomously. The client should see a roadmap item here, not a red flag.
Routed to Roadmap H1Enables OPP-02 autonomyAppears in Report 05 as planned initiative

T2
Modernize the dominant retail-EDI channel now, vs limited stabilization capacity
Strategic tension · direction vs capacityClarificationAccepted
Open question about
The pace of EDI middleware modernization within the stabilization window
The tension
Evidence shows retail EDI is now the dominant channel and structurally under-served — pulling toward immediate modernization. But the StrategyProfile constrains change capacity during stabilization and places app rationalization at H3. Pursue middleware modernization now, or defer?
"67.3% of order volume now flows through retail EDI (Carrefour, Boots, dm, Lidl, Mercadona, Coop), against an O2C process originally designed for pharma-distributor relationships — a structural shift the controlled procedures have not caught up with."
Surfaced by: Order Flow Analysis Export 2025 · EDI Integration Register · Retail Customer Onboarding Guide
SME
SME note · known and accepted
The horizon split already settles this. Low-disruption exception automation (OPP-02) goes in H1; the heavier middleware modernization waits for H2/H3 so we respect the limited change capacity during stabilization. The direction holds — only the sequencing bends to the constraint, so there's nothing here to flag to the client as a conflict.
