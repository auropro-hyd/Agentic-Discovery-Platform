Transformation Recommendation

Which opportunities to pursue, in what order, by value and feasibility.

1Transformation intent

Opella Europe operates a single, governed Order-to-Cash backbone in which SAP S/4HANA is the undisputed system of record for all customer master data and the EDI channel is fully covered by documented process, control, and monitoring. The four-account credit overstatement totalling €1,450,000 is eliminated through an automated weekly reconciliation engine. EDI — which today carries 5,667 orders (67.3% of volume) yet is explicitly excluded from the Order Management SOP and O2C RACI — is brought inside the formal procedure set with named owners, a controlled dispute path, and proactive anomaly detection. All 14 EDI connections are migrated to Opella Digital management, ending the 24–48 hour Sanofi TSA incident dependency on the highest-revenue accounts. Carrefour France moves to same-day SLA by end of H1. SAP S/4HANA holds 340 clean, de-duplicated customer records, fully mirrored in CRM, with an onboarding guide that names a system-of-record hierarchy. The locked strategy profile — Consolidate + Modernise, H1 AI/governance, H3 app rationalisation — governs scope and pace throughout.

1.1Guiding principles

P1
Strategic direction
Stabilise-then-scale: close the governance and data-integrity gaps that already drive customer-facing failures before extending standardised process coverage to the channel that carries the majority of order volume.

R
01
Establish SAP S/4HANA as Sole Credit Data Authority
Critical PriorityAutomation Pipeline · AIAddresses PP-01  |  Delivers OPP-01

The Credit Management Policy (OPS-EU-FIN-POL-002, §4.1) designates SAP S/4HANA as the single authoritative system of record for all customer credit limits. Four accounts carry CRM credit limits above the ERP-authoritative value, totalling €1,450,000 in total CRM overstatement. Carrefour France is the largest individual gap at €600,000 (CRM €2.4M vs ERP €1.8M). An automated weekly reconciliation engine classifies each discrepancy cause — migration artifact, AM override, or system drift — and routes it to the Credit Controller for action, eliminating the need for manual spreadsheet reconciliation.

Phased actions

  • H1 · Week 1–2Credit Controller (Raj Patel) to complete immediate reconciliation of the four known discrepant accounts — Carrefour France, Boots UK, E.Leclerc, dm. For each: confirm the ERP S/4HANA limit as authoritative; obtain Finance Director written approval for any required ERP adjustment; update CRM to match ERP; document the remediation in the credit management log per OPS-EU-FIN-POL-002 §4.2.
  • H1 · Month 1–2Extend reconciliation to the top 50 revenue accounts. Implement interim governance rule: any CRM credit limit field update requires a Credit Controller email approval record before the change takes effect. CS Team Lead to communicate this to CS team and Account Managers with explicit reference to policy breach consequence in OPS-EU-FIN-POL-002 §5.3.
  • H1 · Month 2–4Resolve the Boots UK payment terms conflict (ERP: NET45, CRM: NET30). Commercial team and Credit Controller to confirm the contractually agreed terms; update both systems. Conduct a systematic payment terms review for all accounts where ERP and CRM terms differ.
  • H2 · Month 4–8Deploy automated ERP/CRM credit reconciliation pipeline (OPP-01): scheduled nightly comparison of credit limits and payment terms; materiality threshold alerts (>€50K variance or any terms mismatch) routed to Credit Controller queue; automated CRM correction workflow with mandatory Credit Controller approval token before update executes.

Success criteria

Zero ERP/CRM credit discrepancies >€50K for top 50 accounts by end H1100% of CRM credit updates carry documented Credit Controller approvalAutomated reconciliation pipeline live by end H2Zero payment terms conflicts for active accounts
Dependency
R-01 (credit data accuracy) is prerequisite for R-04 (EDI monitoring credit-hold pattern). R-02 (EDI SOP) enables R-04 (alert routing logic). R-03 (TSA migration) expands R-04 monitoring coverage to all 14 connections over H1–H3.
R
02
Author & Publish EDI SOP + RACI Addendum
Critical Priority · Quick WinProcess GovernanceAddresses PP-02  |  Delivers OPP-02

The Q3 2025 commitment to publish an EDI SOP and RACI addendum — made in both OPS-EU-CS-SOP-001 (§1.3) and OPS-EU-CS-RACI-001 (version history) — remains unfulfilled as of November 2025. The materials needed are already in the discovery pack: the CS Working Notes (OPS-EU-IT-WN-001) provide the complete operational procedure; the Sanofi SOP v4.0 §11 provides the structural template; the EDI Integration Register (OPS-EU-IT-REG-001) provides connection-specific technical detail. No new process design is required — only documentation and governance. This is a 6-week deliverable with no IT dependency, making it the programme's highest-impact quick win.

Phased actions

  • H1 · Week 1–3Draft the EDI Order Management SOP using: (1) Alina Kovacs's working notes (OPS-EU-IT-WN-001) as the primary procedural source; (2) Sanofi SOP v4.0 §11 as structural reference; (3) EDI Integration Register §3–§5 for connection-specific escalation paths. The SOP must cover: EDI order receipt and SAP integration; credit hold interaction; duplicate order detection; mapping error handling; TSA-managed connection escalation protocol; Sanofi IT helpdesk contact definitions.
  • H1 · Week 3–4CS Team Lead (Alina Kovacs) review and amendment; Opella Digital (James Okafor) technical accuracy review for EDI platform specifics and TSA governance process. Resolve any discrepancies between working notes and the EDI register's governance requirements.
  • H1 · Week 4–5Finance Director and VP Commercial Operations approval. Publish as the EDI addendum to OPS-EU-CS-SOP-001 (reference: OPS-EU-CS-SOP-001-EDI v1.0). Simultaneously update the RACI Matrix (OPS-EU-CS-RACI-001) with EDI-specific accountability rows for: EDI order monitoring, EDI incident escalation (Opella Digital-managed), EDI incident escalation (TSA-managed), credit hold resolution, and EDI-specific monthly reporting.
  • H1 · Week 5–6Brief all CS Representatives and Account Managers on the published SOP. Retire OPS-EU-IT-WN-001 as the primary EDI reference — it may be retained as supplementary background but must not be used as the operational procedure. Confirm James Okafor has accepted the TSA escalation protocol as documented.

Success criteria

03
Execute TSA EDI Connection Migration Programme
High PriorityProgramme · H1→H3Addresses PP-03  |  Delivers OPP-03

Six of Opella's 14 EDI connections remain under Sanofi Shared Services (TSA) management, creating a 24–48 hour incident resolution SLA dependency on five of the domain's highest-revenue accounts. The migration programme is the only recommendation that cannot complete in H1 — but it must begin in H1 to meet the EDI Register's existing (and already-missed) target dates. The Carrefour France connection (#9) had a Q4 2025 migration target; it is already overdue. TSA Schedule Annex C is the first document to obtain — it governs the SLA terms and migration conditions. Carrefour France is the H1 priority; Boots UK and dm follow in H2; E.Leclerc, Lidl, and Coop complete in H3.

Phased actions

  • H1 · Month 1Obtain TSA Schedule Annex C — the contractual document governing SLA terms and migration conditions for all 6 Sanofi-managed connections. Assign a named migration lead for each of the 6 connections. This must happen in Month 1; without Annex C, no migration can be scoped or committed.
  • H1 · Month 1–3Plan and execute the Carrefour France (#9) migration to Opella Digital. This connection had a Q4 2025 target date that has already been missed. Priority rationale: highest single-account revenue exposure; 24–48h TSA SLA creates the largest operational risk. Target: Carrefour France migrated and on Opella Digital SLA by end of Month 3.
  • H1 · Month 2–6Begin E.Leclerc (#12) migration planning; develop detailed H2 migration schedule for Boots UK (#10) and dm (#11) with technical scoping, Sanofi coordination plan, and customer communication template.
  • H2 · Month 6–12Complete Boots UK (#10) and dm (#11) migration. Begin scoping for remaining connections — E.Leclerc (#12), Lidl Europe (#13), Coop Group (#14) — and execute based on complexity and commercial priority order.
  • H3 · Month 12–18+Complete remaining TSA connection migrations. Formal TSA closure: confirm with Sanofi Shared Services that all six connections are now Opella Digital-managed; archive the TSA EDI governance record.

Success criteria

TSA Schedule Annex C obtained and reviewed by end of Month 1Named leads assigned for all 6 connections by end of Month 1Carrefour France migrated to Opella Digital by end of Month 3All 6 TSA connections on Opella Digital SLA by end of H3
R
04
Deploy EDI Monitoring & Seasonal Coverage Protocol
Medium PriorityAI · OperationsAddresses PP-04  |  Delivers OPP-04

Order flow analysis identifies a systematic EDI fulfilment rate degradation to approximately 59% during the April–May and October–November cluster windows against a standard baseline of 91% — a 32-percentage-point gap. The 19 stockout customer complaints in 2025 cluster periods represent active relationship risk. This recommendation deploys proactive monitoring with 5 detection patterns (acknowledgement timeout, duplicate orders, mapping error clusters, fulfilment rate degradation, credit holds) and implements TSA-aware alert routing: 8 Opella Digital-managed connections route to James Okafor; 6 Sanofi-managed connections route to the Sanofi IT helpdesk with a commercial escalation to Sophie Marchetti. Target: P-01 and P-02 patterns live by week 6.

Phased actions

  • H1 · Month 1–2Configure EDI health monitoring alerts on the Opella Digital platform for Opella-managed connections 1–8. Alert thresholds: EDI fulfilment rate <85% over any rolling 48-hour window; ORDRSP timeout rate >5% on any single connection; any connection with zero ORDERS received for >4 hours during business hours. Route all P1 alerts directly to James Okafor; CC Alina Kovacs.
  • H1 · Month 2Define and publish the Seasonal EDI Coverage Protocol for the April–May and October–November windows: activate manual order backup pre-authorisation for the five highest-EDI-volume accounts; set daily EDI health check cadence (vs. reactive-only); CS Team Lead to hold a pre-season briefing with CS team and James Okafor before each window. TSA-managed connections (9–14): alert via Sanofi IT helpdesk as per current working notes, with commercial escalation to Sophie Marchetti for P1 events.
  • H2 · Month 6–9Integrate AI anomaly detection (OPP-04): train on 2025 order flow data to identify early indicators of fulfilment rate decline (mapping error frequency, ORDRSP timeout clustering, specific account order volume drops). Alert CS Team Lead 24–48 hours before fulfilment rate reaches the alert threshold. TSA-managed connection monitoring extended as each connection migrates to Opella Digital under R-03.

Success criteria

EDI fulfilment ≥ 85% in all calendar windows including Apr–May and Oct–NovCustomer stockout escalations <5 per quarterP1 EDI alerts reach James Okafor within 15 minutes of threshold breachAI anomaly detection live on Opella-managed connections by end H2
R
05
Execute Customer Master Deduplication & Governance
Medium PriorityDataAddresses PP-05  |  Delivers OPP-05

SAP S/4HANA holds 340 active customer records; SAP CRM holds 318 — a difference of 22 accounts that exist in the ERP but are not reflected in CRM. Six accounts migrated from the Sanofi legacy customer master in May 2024 carry different credit limits and payment terms in each system. The Retail Customer Onboarding Guide instructs the CS Team Lead to "create the customer record in all systems" without naming which systems are authoritative or defining a hierarchy when records diverge. This recommendation executes a one-time deduplication and alignment exercise, resolves the 22 ERP-only gaps, eliminates the 6 duplicate records, and establishes an onboarding governance protocol naming SAP S/4HANA as the authoritative master for all new accounts.

Phased actions

  • H1 · Month 1–2Extract the 22 ERP-only accounts. For each: determine whether CRM is used in commercial operations for that account; where yes, create the CRM record populated from ERP master data; where no, document the deliberate exclusion decision and confirm with the relevant Account Manager. Target: 100% ERP/CRM account count alignment for active accounts.
  • H1 · Month 1–2Resolve the 6 Sanofi-migrated duplicate records: confirm the ERP record as the single authoritative master; archive or correct the CRM record to mirror ERP; document the resolution in both systems. These six accounts overlap with the four PP-01 discrepant accounts — the credit limit reconciliation under R-01 covers this simultaneously.
  • H1 · Month 2–3Publish the CRM update governance protocol (aligned with R-01 approval workflow): all credit limit and payment terms field updates in CRM require Credit Controller written approval before execution; account setup in CRM for any new account must reference the ERP customer number; CS Team Lead to review CRM account completeness quarterly against ERP as part of the existing O2C self-assessment.

Success criteria

100% ERP/CRM account count alignment for active accounts by end H1Zero duplicate records for Sanofi-migrated accounts by end Month 2CRM update governance protocol published and adopted by CS teamQuarterly ERP/CRM count check embedded in O2C self-assessment
Dependency
R-05 depends on R-01: the ERP/CRM governance framework and CRM update approval workflow established under R-01 is the control mechanism R-05 relies on to prevent new data divergence after deduplication.
