Transformation Roadmap

The recommended opportunities, sequenced across three horizons.

Strategic direction. Stabilise-then-scale: close the governance and data-integrity gaps that already drive customer-facing failures before extending standardised process coverage to the channel that carries the majority of order volume.

1Implementation roadmap

Master programme — 7 workstreams across H1 (0–6m), H2 (6–18m), H3 (18–36+ months)

2Horizon detail

H1 — Stabilise data integrity and visibility on the highest-exposure failures (0-6 months)

  • R-02: Author EDI SOP & RACI addendum — Quick Win weeks 1–4 — The Q3 2025 commitment remains unfulfilled. All source materials exist in the discovery pack. Alina Kovacs owns delivery. Target: published and briefed to the CS team by week 4 with no IT dependency.
  • R-05 + OPP-01: Customer master dedup and credit engine MVP weeks 3–12 — Resolve 22 ERP-only accounts and 6 duplicate records (R-05). OPP-01 weekly reconciliation report live by week 8; first report delivered to Raj Patel with Carrefour France €600,000 discrepancy flagged for manual correction.
  • R-03: Obtain TSA Annex C and begin Carrefour France migration weeks 2–24 — Annex C is the prerequisite for all migration scoping. Carrefour France (#9) is the H1 target — overdue from Q4 2025. James Okafor owns. TSA inventory for remaining 5 connections complete by month 5.
  • OPP-04: EDI anomaly detection MVP weeks 3–6 — P-01 (acknowledgement timeout) and P-02 (duplicate detection) patterns live by week 6 with TSA-aware routing. Critical: October–November seasonal window must be covered. P-03 through P-05 in H1 months 3–4.

H2 — Extend governed process coverage to the channels that move the volume (6-18 months)

  • Bring EDI into the official Order Management SOP and RACI opportunity — EDI carries 67.3% of orders (5,667) and 66.8% of value (EUR 59,711,399.53) but is explicitly excluded from both the SOP and the O2C RACI, which today cover only manual telephone and email channels. Formalising ownership for the majority channel removes the structural cause of unprocessed orders.
  • Reduce dependency on third-party EDI connection support opportunity — Working notes record that several EDI connections rely on the Sanofi IT helpdesk with a 24-to-48-hour turnaround, which lengthens resolution on a channel already producing the largest share of order value. Bringing this support in-scope shortens the path to fix failed orders.
  • Embed master-data controls into customer onboarding and change opportunity — With the H1 reconciliation complete, preventing recurrence requires controls at the point of account creation and change so the 267-account mismatch condition does not rebuild. The onboarding guide and credit policy provide the standard to enforce.

H3 — Structural transformation: TSA migration complete, AI automation gate (18–36+ months)

  • R-03 Phase 3: Complete remaining TSA connection migrations programme — E.Leclerc (#12), Lidl Europe (#13), and Coop Group (#14) migrate to Opella Digital management. All 14 EDI connections under Opella control; Sanofi TSA dependency fully retired.
  • H2 AI Gate: Assess automated CRM write-back from OPP-01 decision gate — If OPP-01 reconciliation engine has sustained ≥95% accuracy across 3+ months, the Credit Controller may approve automated CRM write-back for small discrepancies. If accuracy or governance confidence is insufficient, defer to H4.
  • OPP-04 mature: Full seasonal coverage and TSA-connected monitoring AI · operations — OPP-04 monitoring extended to all migrated TSA connections as R-03 completes. Seasonal threshold parameters tuned based on two full April–May and Oct–Nov windows of operational data.
