Which Domain for the Demo?

Opella lens: newly independent FMCH company, pharma-grade inherited infrastructure, trying to move at consumer-goods speed. Which domain dramatises that tension most sharply for a Head of Strategy?

A
Order-to-Cash (O2C)
Customer order → fulfilment → invoice → cash collection across pharmacy, retail, distributor, e-commerce
What the platform surfaces
  • Customer master data inherited from Sanofi — shared vs. Opella-specific records unclear
  • Credit management and AR systems: Sanofi's SAP vs. standalone need
  • Channel complexity: pharmacy, grocery, e-commerce, distributor each with different order flows
  • Country-specific invoicing and tax compliance requirements
  • EDI integrations with major retail chains — inherited, owned, or TSA-managed?
Opella-specific tension
  • Sanofi's O2C was pharma-focused: prescription channel, GPO contracts, specialty distributors
  • Opella's O2C is consumer-focused: retail velocity, promotional pricing, shelf availability
  • The mismatch between what was inherited and what FMCH requires is immediately visible
  • TSA exit means every inherited Sanofi O2C system is a countdown clock
Strong audience resonance Clear TSA exit urgency Good cross-system complexity Medium differentiation from generic demo
The story
"Your O2C process was designed for pharma channels. Now you're selling Doliprane at Carrefour and Allegra through Amazon. The handoffs, the systems, the data — none of it was built for this. Here's what that actually looks like."
B
Procure-to-Pay (P2P)
Purchase requisition → PO → goods receipt → supplier invoice → payment across direct and indirect procurement
What the platform surfaces
  • Procurement systems likely still under TSA from Sanofi (Ariba, Coupa, or similar)
  • Supplier master data: which are Opella-only vs. shared Sanofi suppliers?
  • 13 manufacturing sites each with local procurement variations
  • Indirect procurement (IT, marketing, facilities) heavily fragmented post-carve-out
  • Contract management: who owns supplier contracts now that Sanofi has separated?
Opella-specific tension
  • Every TSA procurement system is a liability — time-limited access to Sanofi's stack
  • Manufacturing procurement (APIs, packaging) is GMP-regulated — compliance complexity
  • New CD&R ownership likely means procurement efficiency is a top-3 value creation lever
  • But this is inherently back-office — harder to make viscerally urgent for a strategy audience
TSA exit driver CD&R value creation angle Lower audience resonance Back-office feel — hard to dramatise
The problem with P2P for this demo
A Head of Strategy understands P2P matters, but it doesn't create a "lean forward" moment. It's compliance and cost reduction — important, but not the story that makes them say "we need this now." Save this for a follow-on domain sprint, not the opening demo.
C
Demand Planning & Distribution Management Recommended
Demand sensing → S&OP → production planning → DC distribution → pharmacy/retail last mile, 38 countries, ~100 brands, seasonal peaks
What the platform surfaces
  • Demand planning tools inherited from Sanofi — pharma-grade (SAP IBP or equivalent), wrong fit for FMCH speed
  • S&OP process: Commercial forecasts vs. Operations capacity vs. Finance targets — three teams, three systems, no single source of truth
  • Regional distribution variation: EU centralised DC vs. North America direct-to-retailer vs. APAC distributor model
  • Seasonal demand spikes: Allegra in spring, Doliprane/Dulcolax year-round, cold remedies in winter — demand signal latency visible in system evidence
  • Serialisation/traceability: pharma-grade requirements (Sanofi legacy) vs. FMCH actual regulatory need country by country
Opella-specific tension
  • Sanofi's supply chain was built for batch pharmaceutical manufacturing — long cycles, strict GMP, heavy compliance tooling
  • Opella needs FMCH-speed: promotional surges, shelf-fill velocity, retailer service level agreements
  • 100 brands × 38 countries × seasonal patterns = complexity that no human team can map manually
  • A stockout of Allegra in allergy season or Doliprane during a flu wave = direct brand and revenue damage — the audience feels this
  • CD&R will want working capital efficiency: inventory turns, days-in-DC, demand accuracy — all traceable to this domain
Highest audience resonance Most Opella-specific Rich cross-system complexity Emotional urgency (stockouts = brand damage) CD&R value creation angle built-in
The story
"You have 100 brands, 38 countries, seasonal demand spikes, and a supply chain built by a pharma company. Right now, Commercial forecasts in one system, Operations plans in another, and Finance reconciles in a spreadsheet. No one has a complete picture of where Allegra is going to stock out next spring — until it does. Here's what that looks like when the machine reads it all at once."
My recommendation: C — but thread O2C as the downstream handoff.
Demand Planning & Distribution is where Opella's inherited pharma complexity vs. FMCH reality is most visible and most emotionally urgent for a strategy audience. O2C appears as the downstream consequence — when demand planning fails, O2C breaks. Showing that cross-domain link in the demo makes it feel like a real landscape, not an isolated process.
