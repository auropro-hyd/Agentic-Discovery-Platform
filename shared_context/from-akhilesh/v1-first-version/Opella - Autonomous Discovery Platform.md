## Problem Statement

Opella (global FMCH — Fast Moving Consumer Healthcare, spun from a large parent organisation) inherited 1000+ applications post-de-merger. Many of these applications overlap across teams and functions. Any consolidation, automation, or AI enablement initiative requires a discovery phase that currently takes 2–3 months of business team time — interviews, workshops, and manual documentation review.

They cannot give that time. The transformation team and Head of Strategy need a roadmap for what to do with this landscape, but the traditional discovery model is too expensive in stakeholder hours.

**Three live pain points (all confirmed in the client call):**
1. Timeline pressure — board/C-suite expects a consolidation plan; the data to build it doesn't exist yet
2. Operational disruption risk — afraid of decommissioning the wrong systems and breaking live processes
3. Business stakeholder time cost — discovery requires pulling people off their real work for months

## Demand Evidence

- Client explicitly stated they "cannot give the time of business teams for discovery"
- Opella has 1000+ applications inherited from parent organisation, significant overlap across teams
- AuroPro has prior engagement data anchoring the 80% effort reduction claim — specific discovery engagements where manual sprint time can be compared against the AI-assisted model
- Client requested a demo in 1 week, indicating urgency and intent to move

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Who:** Head of Strategy + Transition Team Head + their leads at Opella (business/strategy audience, not technical evaluators)

**What they care about:** Decisions and timelines. They want to walk out of the demo saying "this is what we've been waiting for" — not understand how the agents work.

**The wedge — a single live end-to-end flow using synthetic FMCH data across the full input stack:**

**Inputs ingested:**
1. Process documents and SOPs
2. Application walkthrough guides and how-to documentation
3. Telemetry insights — synthesised by a dedicated Telemetry Intelligence Agent from logs, traces, and audit trails (all telemetry access follows necessary compliance)
4. App crawling in non-prod environments (all apps assumed to have non-prod) — complementary verification layer to confirm what the documents describe is still accurate

**Processing flow:**
1. Evidence Synthesis Agent — extracts entities, systems, processes, decision points, handoffs, and dependencies across all inputs
2. Domain Analysis Agent — maps domain boundaries, identifies process hotspots and ownership boundaries
3. Gap Detection — surfaces what is mentioned but unverifiable, what is missing, what is ambiguous, what conflicts between document evidence and actual app behaviour; gaps are surfaced to the AuroPro SME via the Discovery Copilot for resolution
4. Readiness Agent — assesses the technology and data landscape for AI enablement readiness

**Gap resolution loop:**
When a gap is detected, it is surfaced to the AuroPro SME through the Discovery Copilot. The SME conducts a targeted clarification (not a full workshop) to resolve the gap — either by querying the client's transformation team or by running a targeted app crawl to verify. This keeps the 20% client involvement focused on decisions, not data gathering.

**Discovery outputs (full suite):**
- **Current State Assessment** — domain boundaries, process hotspots, system context, ownership map
- **Pain Points & Opportunity Report** — validated issues with business impact framing and feasibility notes
- **Transformation Recommendation** — for each identified opportunity, a recommended intervention type:
  - Modernisation (replace/consolidate)
  - Automation pipeline (workflow automation for repeatable processes)
  - AI Agent (autonomous task execution)
  - Human-in-the-loop agentic workflow (AI-assisted with human sign-off gates)
- **AI Opportunity Portfolio** — prioritised initiatives with value drivers, effort estimates, and sequencing
- **Transformation Roadmap** — phased delivery plan with dependencies, risk flags, and success measures
- **Supporting Artefacts** — process maps, data flow diagrams, application dependency charts, and decision rationale for each recommended initiative

AuroPro builds the roadmap in collaboration with the transformation team. The client's transformation team reviews and signs off on all outputs.

## Premises (confirmed)

1. **AI trusted for pattern detection; SME validates exceptions and business context.** The platform's AI is authoritative on synthesis at scale. The AuroPro SME interprets ambiguous findings, validates business implications, and owns the judgment calls. The client trusts the output because AuroPro's expert has reviewed it — but AI handles the volume.

2. **80% effort reduction is anchored to prior engagement data.** Not an industry estimate. AuroPro has run manual discovery sprints and can compare time-effort against the AI-assisted model. The claim needs a specific anchor ready: "In our prior engagements, domain discovery for a single operational area took X weeks of business team involvement. Our platform compresses that to Y, with the client only needed for review and sign-off."

3. **Confidence-tiered output is the right design.** Apps/processes verified by document evidence AND non-prod crawl = high-confidence findings. Apps/processes with document evidence only = amber, flagged for SME review. Gaps (mentioned in docs but unverifiable or contradicted by app behaviour) = red risk flags. This is visible in the output — the client can see exactly what's verified vs. what needs human judgment.

4. **All apps have usable non-prod environments.** Telemetry access follows necessary compliance. These are not open questions — they are engagement pre-conditions.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Single live flow (SELECTED)
**Summary:** One working end-to-end pipeline using synthetic FMCH data. Evidence Synthesis + Domain Analysis + Gap Detection + full Discovery Outputs suite.
**Effort:** S (3–4 days to build the working flow)
**Risk:** Low — synthetic data means full control, zero demo-day surprises
**Pros:**
- One thing working end-to-end beats five things partially working
- Gap detection surfaced to SME via Discovery Copilot is the "aha moment" — shows the human-AI loop working, not just pattern detection
- Confidence-tiering demonstrated in real-time output
- Full output suite (roadmap, opportunity portfolio, intervention types) shows the end-to-end value
**Cons:**
- Shows one domain only — full 1000+ app scope is positioned as the engagement delivery, not the demo
**Reuses:** Evidence ingestion patterns from prior AuroPro discovery work

### Approach B: Multi-agent staged demo
**Summary:** Live document ingestion + pre-computed downstream agent outputs presented as a continuous pipeline
**Effort:** M (5–6 days, higher prep time)
**Risk:** Medium — pre-computed outputs break if stakeholder asks to rerun on different input
**Pros:** Gives impression of full suite including Opportunity Prioritization output
**Cons:** Brittle; any deviation from the scripted path exposes it; higher demo risk

### Approach C: Output-first dashboard demo
**Summary:** Build the output UI first, show pre-generated reports, explain the agent pipeline conceptually
**Effort:** S (4–5 days focused on UI quality)
**Risk:** Low technically, high on credibility
**Pros:** Fastest build, polished outputs
**Cons:** No live AI visible; strategy audience will ask "can we see it process something?"

## Recommended Approach

**Approach A with gap surfacing and the full output suite as the centrepiece.**

Why: The transformation team's specific fear is decommissioning the wrong systems and not having a coherent plan for the 1000+ app landscape. The demo addresses both: gap detection shows them what's risky and unverifiable, and the full output suite (roadmap, opportunity portfolio, intervention types) shows them that the platform doesn't just describe the problem — it generates the plan to address it.

**Demo narrative (three acts):**

**Act 1 — Name the pain (60 seconds)**
"Your transformation team is facing 2–3 months of discovery for each initiative you want to tackle. That's not a people problem — it's a data problem. You don't have a machine-readable map of what you own, how it's used, and where the dependencies are. That's what takes 3 months. Here's what happens when the machine does 80% of that work."

**Act 2 — Show the capability (10–12 minutes)**
- Upload a synthetic FMCH SOP — recommended domain: **Consumer Safety & Pharmacovigilance Reporting** (or Regulatory Submissions & Product Lifecycle, depending on which resonates most with Opella's current transition focus; confirm before building the synthetic data set)
- Evidence Synthesis runs live — entities, systems, processes, decision points, handoffs extracted across documents, walkthrough guides, and synthesised telemetry
- Domain Analysis builds the process map — audience sees their landscape take shape
- App crawling verification runs against a synthetic non-prod environment — shows where document claims are confirmed vs. contradicted
- Gap Detection fires and surfaces to Discovery Copilot — highlight 3–5 specific gaps: "This process references System X but we found no evidence of active usage in telemetry or app walkthrough. Is it still in use, or is it a shadow system from the parent org? Flagged for SME clarification."
- Show confidence-tiering: green (verified by docs + crawl), amber (doc-only, needs review), red (gap/conflict)
- Present the full output suite: Current State Assessment → Pain Points → Intervention Recommendations (modernise / automate / agent / HITL workflow) → AI Opportunity Portfolio → Transformation Roadmap

**Act 3 — The offer (2 minutes)**
"What you just saw ran on synthetic data structured like an FMCH post-merger landscape. In the pilot, we run this against one real domain of your actual landscape — your documents, your apps, your telemetry. You'll see your real gaps, your real risks, and your real transformation roadmap — in days, not months. Your transformation team's role is review and sign-off, not data gathering."

## Open Questions

**For the demo build (resolve in next 48 hours):**
- **Which synthetic FMCH domain?** Client is Opella (global FMCH — consumer health brands). Claims Processing does not resonate. Recommended: **Consumer Safety & Pharmacovigilance Reporting** (process-heavy, regulated, multi-system, FMCH-specific) or **Regulatory Submissions & Product Lifecycle Management** (document-rich, clear handoffs, global market relevance). Confirm which domain maps closer to where Opella's transformation pain is concentrated.
- How is confidence-tiering represented visually in the output? Recommended: colour-coded badges per finding (green/amber/red) with an explanation key — needs a design decision before building
- What does the "gap surfaced to SME via Discovery Copilot" look like in the UI? A notification? A flagged item in the output with an action prompt? Needs to be specified in the demo script

**For the Opella engagement:**
- Which operational domain does Opella want to prioritise first in the pilot?
- Are there any domains that are off-limits or politically sensitive for the initial sprint?

## Success Criteria

**Demo (1 week):**
- Head of Strategy and Transition Team Head leave the meeting saying "we want to do the pilot"
- At least one stakeholder asks "can we run this on one of our actual domains?" — that's the close signal
- The roadmap output lands as credible, not generic — they should be able to see how it maps to their situation

**Pilot phase:**
- Platform processes one real Opella domain and produces a Current State Assessment + Transformation Roadmap the transformation team validates as accurate within one 2-hour review workshop
- Gap detection surfaces at least 3 findings the client confirms as real issues they didn't know about
- AuroPro builds the final roadmap artefact; Opella's transformation team reviews and signs off

**Platform (ongoing):**
- Per engagement: 80% reduction in client stakeholder time vs. manual discovery (anchored to prior engagement benchmarks)
- Full output suite delivered per domain sprint: Current State Assessment, Pain Points & Opportunity Report, Transformation Recommendation, AI Opportunity Portfolio, Transformation Roadmap, Supporting Artefacts

## Distribution Plan

Not a SaaS product. Delivered as a configured engagement framework:
- Phase 1 (Demo): AuroPro-hosted environment, synthetic FMCH data
- Phase 2 (Pilot): AuroPro-hosted with client data under NDA/data agreement; one domain, one 2-hour validation workshop
- Phase 3 (Engagement): Full domain-by-domain discovery sprint, AuroPro-managed delivery, SME-led validation loops; roadmap built by AuroPro, signed off by client transformation team

Commercial model: discovery sprint fixed-fee per domain + optional ongoing monitoring/refresh tier for landscape changes post-consolidation

## The Assignment

Before you write a single line of code, write a 2-page demo script. Not slides — a script. Every screen state, what you say, what the audience sees, what they're supposed to feel. The script is your build spec. It forces every technical decision to be made before you build, not during. Share the script with your team and anyone who's going to be in that room. If someone reads it and says "I don't know what the gap surfaced in Discovery Copilot looks like on screen" — that's a design gap you need to close before building, not during the demo.

The script also tells you what the demo *doesn't* need. Anything not in the script doesn't get built this week.

## What I noticed about how you think

- You said "it is not realistic to have all apps in one shot" — immediately. You didn't sell the platform's ambition at the expense of delivery realism. That's the thinking that keeps engagements from blowing up.
- When I pushed on the SME role, you gave the nuanced answer rather than defending one extreme. "AI handles pattern detection, SME handles exceptions and business context" is a more honest and defensible position than "fully autonomous" or "AI is just a tool."
- You know why the 80% claim is real — you have prior engagement data. That means when the Head of Strategy pushes back on the number, you can tell a specific story, not cite an industry report.
- Gap detection wasn't on the original capability list when you first described the platform — it came from a follow-up question. That suggests you're thinking about what the client actually needs (risk reduction) more than what sounds impressive (full-suite AI).
- When you revised the output scope, you added the intervention taxonomy (modernisation / agent / agentic / automation) unprompted. That tells me you've already thought about the conversation that happens *after* the discovery — not just "here's what you have" but "here's what you do about it."

## Key Review Findings (2026-05-31)

From the steelman external review — items requiring action before demo day:

1. **Script the data provenance answer.** The findings are specific enough to feel planted. Audience will ask "how did you know to look there?" Need a scripted answer explaining synthetic-data-from-carve-out-patterns, pivoting naturally to pilot.

2. **Finding 1 (DP) needs a business consequence up front.** "Three systems with conflicting data" reads as IT problem. Fix: "Three systems — last Allegra season, which one did they trust?"

3. **Re-frame the 12–15 day lag.** Cannot be derived from document ingestion alone. Re-frame as structural dependency (operational lead time pattern), not a statistical finding from log analysis.

4. **Make the cross-reference principle visible once (Scenario B).** Add: "The formal SOP doesn't mention EDI at all. This only surfaced because we read the CS working notes alongside it."

5. **Fix the close.** Remove "a plan your board can act on." Replace with conditional: "...something structured enough to take to your board, or we'll tell you why we don't."

6. **Have the 80% formulation ready.** Precise version: "8–12 weeks of workshops, 15–20 business team members, 4–8 hours each → two weeks, two review sessions, document access. Compression is in extraction, not validation."

7. **Have a post-pilot path sentence ready for discussion.** Not in the demo — in the 25-minute discussion phase: "If the pilot lands, here's what the full engagement model looks like."
