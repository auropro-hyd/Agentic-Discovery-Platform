Demo Script — Order-to-Cash

Domain: O2C secondary · 20 min demo · 25 min discussion · Rebuilt 2026-06-01 · Guide: demo-script-o2c-guide.md
Use when: the primary Demand Planning demo is not possible, the audience is finance or commercial-led, or the meeting pivots to O2C during discussion.
The output suite is the centrepiece — discovery is the setup. The AI Opportunity Portfolio is the main event. Everything else is context.
Guiding principle — same as primary script
Never say
  • Pipeline, agents, blocks
  • Knowledge Graph
  • Evidence Synthesis
  • Gap detected / gap resolved
  • Nodes and edges
  • Technical architecture
Say instead
  • The platform is reading your landscape
  • Your operational map
  • Here's what it found
  • A finding that needs a quick answer
  • How your processes connect
  • What your business actually looks like
0:00 – 1:30
The Setup (1.5 min) — Name the mismatch, not the technology
Say
"Sanofi's Order-to-Cash was built for hospital distributors and pharmacy chains on GPO contracts. That was the business. Opella's business is Doliprane at Carrefour, Allegra on Amazon, Dulcolax through Boots. The channel has changed fundamentally. The question is whether your systems and your processes have changed with it — or whether you're running a consumer healthcare company on infrastructure designed for a different company entirely. We'll let the platform show you."
On screen
Platform open. 12 input documents visible — business-readable names across five categories: process documentation, policy, system exports, integration records, operational logs. Nothing has run.
Don't say
Don't explain what the platform does or how it works. Don't mention synthetic inputs. "We'll let the platform show you" is the bridge — then click run.
1:30 – 5:00
Discovery Runs (3.5 min) — Let the mismatch surface
Say as it runs
"It's reading your order management documentation, your customer records, your system exports, your escalation logs — building a picture of how orders actually move through your business, who owns each step, which systems are involved, where the handoffs break down. We intentionally left some gaps in these documents. Watch what it finds."
On screen — discovery feed (business language only)
Status messages: "Reading order management documentation… Mapping customer ownership across systems… Cross-referencing documented process against actual order flow… Comparing credit management records with fulfilment data… Analysing EDI integration register against system evidence…" Then findings panel begins populating.
🔴 Finding 1 — Customer Master Data: No Single Source of Truth
Major retail and pharmacy customers — including accounts representing over 40% of European revenue — exist in multiple systems with conflicting credit limits, payment terms, and contact records. Credit decisions are being made against different records depending on who processes the order.
🔴 Finding 2 — 67% of Your Order Volume Is Running Without Governance
Your Order Management SOP describes a process for specialty pharma distributors: manual PO receipt, credit review, picking instruction, dispatch confirmation. System evidence shows 67% of your actual orders now arrive through retail EDI — automated, high-velocity, no manual touchpoints. This flow has no SOP, no RACI, no assigned owner. It is running at scale, undocumented.
🟡 Finding 3 — 6 EDI Integrations with Unverified Ownership
14 EDI connections to Carrefour, Boots, dm, and Leclerc are listed in the integration register. 6 were established under Sanofi Shared Services. No documentation of transfer to Opella at carve-out. These may still be operating under Sanofi TSA — an exposure that has not been mapped before today.
Pause after Finding 2 — let "67%" land "Running without governance" — not "undocumented" Finding 3 is amber — "flagged, not blocking, but a TSA risk you need to own"
5:00 – 8:00
Three Findings, Three Resolutions (3 min) — Fast, targeted, no workshop
Say
"Finding 1 needs one quick answer — whoever owns your customer data governance. The platform has already done the work: it's compared all three systems and ranked which has the most complete, most recently updated records. We confirm the answer and proceed."
On screen — Finding 1 resolution
Three candidate answers ranked by evidence: (1) SAP CRM is authoritative — other systems are stale copies from Sanofi integration; (2) AR system holds live credit decisions — CRM is reference only; (3) No single system is authoritative — parallel updates have diverged. Select option 1. Finding updates: "Resolved — SAP CRM confirmed as customer master system of record."
Say after resolution
"Finding 2 — the undocumented EDI flow — stays in the output as a priority action. That's a process documentation sprint, not a quick call. Finding 3 — the TSA-exposed connections — goes to Digital and Legal. They need to know about this before TSA exit planning goes any further."
Don't say
Don't call this "gap resolution." Don't mention a Copilot or agent. Say "a finding that needed a quick answer." The mechanics are invisible.
Total time here: ~2 min Don't linger — the output suite is what they came to see

Current State Assessment (1.5 min) — Deliverable 1 of 6
①   Current State Assessment
Say
"Here's Deliverable 1 — the Current State Assessment. Three panels: what your process boundaries look like today, which systems are involved and how they connect, and who owns what. This is the operational map the platform built from your documents."
On screen — three assessment panels
Panel A — Domain Map: Two O2C flows side by side. "Documented O2C — Specialty Distributor (33% of volume)" and "Actual O2C — Retail EDI (67% of volume)." Confidence indicators: documented flow amber (process exists, system evidence partial), EDI flow red (no SOP, no RACI, 6 TSA-flagged connections).

Panel B — System Context: Five systems mapped: SAP ERP (order fulfilment), SAP CRM (customer master — confirmed SoT after Finding 1 resolution), OMS (order management), EDI Gateway (14 connections, 6 TSA-flagged), AR System. Each system shows owning team and integration dependencies.

Panel C — Ownership Map: RACI per process step. Credit review: Finance owns for manual orders. EDI credit review: blank — no owner. EDI exception handling: CS team (de facto, not by design). TSA-exposed integrations: owner unverified.
Say — cross-domain note (if primary demo ran)
"And here — a pattern the platform found across domains. When the demand forecast underruns, your O2C escalation queue spikes 12 to 15 days later. Your customer service team is absorbing a demand planning failure. It's in the map."
Name the three panels — don't walk through all of them The two-flow domain map is the key visual — pause on it briefly
9:30 – 11:00
Pain Points & Opportunity Report (1.5 min) — Deliverable 2 of 6
②   Pain Points & Opportunity Report
Say
"Deliverable 2 — every finding quantified. Not a list of issues — a prioritised set of pain points with the business impact figure and a feasibility read on each. The numbers come directly from the source documents the platform read."
€600K+
Customer master conflict — uninstructed credit over-exposure
Carrefour FR: €2.4M credit limit in CRM vs €1.8M agreed limit in ERP — €600K of uninstructed exposure. Boots UK: Net 30 invoiced, Net 45 paid — 15-day payment gap on every invoice cycle. 22 accounts with unresolved credit status across systems.
Feasibility: targeted HITL session — one day to resolve 22 accounts with ranked evidence. Immediate risk reduction.
34 incidents/yr
EDI exception chaos — CS team absorbing an operational burden they don't own
34 manual CS interventions per year on EDI failures. SLA drops 94% → 61% during error clusters. ~370 retail orders miss delivery commitment per cluster event. Carrefour FR threatened Doliprane delisting — documented in escalation log.
Feasibility: automation pipeline — no intelligence required, pure routing and alerting. Independent of Opportunity 1 — can run in parallel from day 1.
6 of 14 EDI
TSA-exposed integrations — unverified ownership from carve-out
6 major retail EDI connections established under Sanofi Shared Services. No transfer documentation at carve-out. CS working notes reference "Sanofi IT helpdesk" as the contact for connection failures — this is the clearest evidence the connections were never transferred.
Feasibility: Legal/Digital action — not an AI opportunity. Flagged for TSA exit planning. Required before Opportunity 2 can own its connection map.
5,665 orders/yr
No credit check on 67% of O2C volume — EDI channel exempt by omission, not design
Credit policy exists for all customer tiers — but covers manual and email orders only. EDI channel (67% of volume, 1.2h avg processing) has no credit gate. O2C RACI has no row for EDI credit review. Zero audit trail for credit exposure on the majority order flow.
Feasibility: AI agent — rules-based, high coverage. Requires Opportunity 1 (reconciled customer master) to complete first.

Opportunity 2
EDI Order Exception Resolution Pipeline
Automation Pipeline Run in Parallel
34 incidents/yr
Manual CS interventions on EDI failures. SLA drops 94% → 61% during error clusters — ~370 retail orders miss delivery commitment per cluster event. Carrefour FR threatened Doliprane delisting. CS team absorbing operational burden of a process they have no ownership of.
Derived from: Doc 06 (CS Escalation Log) · Doc 10 (Order Flow Export) · Doc 11 (CS Working Notes)
Current State
1Retail EDI order arrives from Carrefour / Boots / dm / Leclerc via EDI connection
2EDI connection fails — order not picked up by OMS. No automated alert exists.
3Customer contacts CS team to chase order. CS identifies the failure manually.
4CS re-enters order manually into OMS. Calls Digital team for fix. For 6 Sanofi-managed connections: calls Sanofi IT helpdesk.
5Resolution: multiple days. SLA breached. Escalation logged. "Third time this quarter for Carrefour FR."
Future State
1Retail EDI order arrives. Failure detected automatically at message level — no customer contact required.
2Failed order auto-routed to processing queue — pre-filled from EDI message data. No re-keying by CS.
3Digital team alerted automatically with connection status and failure type. Opella Digital or Sanofi IT notified per connection ownership map.
4CS informed of status — not responsible for resolution. Customer contacted proactively with ETA before they escalate.
5Resolution: hours, not days. SLA impact contained. Escalation pattern broken.
Intervention — Automation Pipeline
Automated detection at the EDI message layer, not the customer complaint layer. Pre-filled routing queue removes manual re-entry. Owner-aware alerting — the pipeline knows whether to contact Opella Digital or Sanofi IT based on the connection ownership map. CS team exits the EDI operations loop entirely.

Opportunity 3
AI Credit Decisioning Agent — Retail EDI Channel
AI Agent Requires Opp 1 First
5,665 orders/yr
Retail EDI orders processed annually with no credit check — 67.3% of total O2C volume. Credit policy exists for all customer tiers but has no coverage for the EDI channel. Zero audit trail for credit exposure on the majority order flow. No RACI owner for EDI credit decisions.
Derived from: Doc 02 (Credit Management Policy) · Doc 03 (O2C RACI) · Doc 10 (Order Flow Export)
Current State
1Manual/email orders: credit checked against customer master per Credit Management Policy before fulfilment
2EDI orders (67% of volume): arrive automated at 1.2h avg — no credit check in the flow. Policy not applied.
3O2C RACI has no row for EDI credit review — no owner, no escalation path, no audit record
4Over-limit or risky EDI orders fulfilled without review. Exposure unquantified. Discovery only in AR ageing report.
Future State
1EDI order arrives — AI agent reads order value, customer identity, channel tier against reconciled customer master (Opp 1 output)
2Known Tier 1/2 accounts within agreed credit limit: auto-approved. Fulfilment proceeds. Audit record written.
3New accounts, over-limit orders, or flagged risk: surfaced to credit team exception queue with evidence and recommended decision
4Credit team reviews exceptions only — not routine approvals. 100% of EDI volume now covered by credit policy.
5Full audit trail per EDI order credit decision. No blind spot in AR exposure.
Intervention — AI Agent
Rules-based credit agent operating on the reconciled customer master from Opportunity 1. Auto-approves routine EDI orders, surfaces exceptions to the credit team with pre-ranked recommendations. Credit team workload shifts from routine approvals to exception handling only. Prerequisite: Opportunity 1 must complete first — the agent cannot function on conflicting credit limits from two systems.
