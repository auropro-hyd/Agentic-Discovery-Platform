1.   Purpose and Scope
1.1   Purpose
This register documents all active Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) connections between the organisation Europe and
its retail and pharmacy trading partners. It records the connection configuration, managing entity, operational
status, and TSA governance status for each connection.
The register provides the authoritative reference for:
- Identifying the management responsibility for each EDI connection.
- Understanding which connections remain subject to the Sanofi Transition Services Agreement (TSA) and
where to direct operational queries.
- Tracking the migration timeline for TSA-managed connections.
- Supporting incident management and customer escalation handling.
1.2   Scope
The register covers all EDI connections between the organisation Europe and external trading partners, including
connections that are currently managed by Sanofi Shared Services under the TSA. It does not cover:
- Web portal or API-based order integrations (documented separately).
- Internal system integrations between the organisation entities.
- EDI connections in markets outside the the organisation Europe commercial region.
- Connections in setup or testing phase (not yet live in production).
1.3   Relationship to Other Documents
This register is referenced in the Order Management SOP (OPS-EU-CS-SOP-001, Section 1.3) and the EDI
Dispute Resolution Working Notes (OPS-EU-IT-WN-001). Operational queries regarding specific connections
should be directed per the contact information in Section 5.
2.   Architecture Overview
2.1   EDI Platform
the organisation Digital manages active EDI connections through the the organisation Europe EDI platform (IBM Sterling B2B
Integrator, hosted on the organisation private cloud, Frankfurt). All the organisation Digital-managed connections in this register are
routed through this platform.
Connections managed by Sanofi Shared Services remain on the Sanofi EDI platform (SAP Integration Suite,
managed by Sanofi Global IT) under the terms of the TSA. These connections will be migrated to the the organisation
platform per the migration schedule in Section 4.

4.   TSA-Managed Connections - Transition Status
4.1   TSA Governance
Connections 9-14 in Section 3 are operated by Sanofi Shared Services under the terms of the Transition
Services Agreement (TSA) between the organisation and Sanofi. Under the TSA, Sanofi continues to provide EDI
connectivity services for these six trading partners until each connection is formally transferred to the organisation Digital
management.
The specific service terms, SLAs, handover process, and migration timeline for these connections are defined in
TSA Schedule Annex C. TSA Schedule Annex C is a separate controlled document held by the the organisation TSA
Programme Office and is not reproduced in this register.
  TSA SCHEDULE ANNEX C
The terms governing connections 9-14 are set out in TSA Schedule Annex C.
For questions about SLAs, incident response, or migration timelines for
TSA-managed connections, contact the the organisation TSA Programme Office.
DO NOT contact Sanofi IT directly for operational issues on these connections
without first notifying the TSA Programme Office - all contact with Sanofi
Shared Services must be managed through the TSA governance framework.

5.2   TSA-Managed Connections (9-14)
For connections managed by Sanofi Shared Services (connections 9-14), incident and support escalation must
follow the TSA governance process. CS Representatives and CS Team Lead must NOT contact Sanofi IT
directly. All incidents must be reported to the the organisation Digital team, who will escalate through the TSA framework.
- First contact: the organisation Digital (J. Okafor)  -  who will determine whether the issue is the organisation-side or
Sanofi-side and escalate accordingly.
- For Sanofi-side issues: the organisation Digital submits a TSA incident ticket to Sanofi Shared Services EDI
support. SLAs are governed by TSA Schedule Annex C.
- Escalation for unresolved TSA incidents: the organisation TSA Programme Office.
6.   Register Maintenance and Governance
This register is reviewed quarterly by the organisation Digital. Changes to connection status, management ownership, or
TSA classification must be notified to the EDI Integration Lead within five business days of the change taking
effect. The following types of change trigger an immediate (unscheduled) update:
- Addition or decommission of a production connection.
- Transfer of a connection from TSA to the organisation Digital management.
- Material change to a connection's message types or technical configuration.
