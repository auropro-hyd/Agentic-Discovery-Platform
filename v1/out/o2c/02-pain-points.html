2Root cause

How the issues found trace back to shared structural causes:

How the issues trace back to shared causes
Left: structural causes. Right: the pain points each one drives.

No enforced single source of record. Both the credit/master-data issues (PP3, PP4) and the EDI governance gap (PP1) stem from authority not being defined or enforced: the credit policy names S/4HANA as authoritative but does not define which system is authoritative in practice, and the dominant order channel has no documented owner.

High-volume activity falling into manual workarounds. EDI carries two-thirds of order volume and value yet sits outside the SOP and RACI, so failures are handled by manual re-entry (PP1, PP2) and feed the largest single category of customer escalations (PP5).

Major retail accounts repeatedly affected. The same large customers (Carrefour, Lidl, Coop, Mercadona, Boots, dm, E.Leclerc) appear across master-data divergence, unfulfilled orders and escalations, concentrating risk in the most strategic accounts.

PP
01
EDI order failures drop into an undocumented manual gap
High SeverityOrder Management / Channel …

EDI is the dominant order channel yet it is explicitly excluded from both the Order Management SOP and the O2C RACI, leaving no documented owner or process when an automated order fails. When EDI orders do not flow through, customer service staff are instructed to manually re-key them as a workaround.

PP
03
Credit limits diverge between CRM and ERP with no authoritative source
High SeverityMaster Data / Credit Manage…

Of 318 matched accounts, the majority hold a different credit limit in CRM than in ERP, and the credit policy does not define which system is authoritative even though policy text asserts S/4HANA should be the sole source of record. The aggregate absolute divergence across accounts is substantial.

Root cause: The credit policy states S/4HANA is the single authoritative source but, in practice, the policy does not define which system is authoritative, allowing two systems to hold conflicting credit limits.

Credit decisions and order releases can be based on the wrong limit, creating credit-risk exposure and disputes.
