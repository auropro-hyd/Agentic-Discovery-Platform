Current State Assessment

How Procure-to-Pay runs today at Acme Manufacturing. A factual baseline of the process, the systems that support it, and how information is structured — stated as fact, no judgements.

1Domain overview

1.1Context and scope

This section documents the current state of the procurement purchase order process, governed by the procurement policy and evidenced by the purchase order export. The policy sets two operative control points: all purchase orders must be raised in the procurement system before goods are ordered, and any purchase order above EUR 50,000 requires approval from the Category Manager and a second approval from Finance before release to the supplier. The export records two distinct approval_status values: approved and single_approval_only.

The process runs from raising a purchase order in the procurement system, through approval routing keyed to PO value, to release to the supplier and ordering of goods. The policy requires the PO to be raised before goods are ordered. POs above EUR 50,000 follow a two-approval path (Category Manager plus Finance); other POs follow a single approval path. The export shows seven POs carrying the status single_approval_only with a combined value of EUR 557,000, representing 12 percent of total PO value, and three POs recorded as po_before_order equal to no with a combined value of EUR 216,000, representing 4.7 percent of total PO value. All three of the po_before_order equal to no POs also carry the single_approval_only status.

3Process inventory

Each stage of the process in detail — how it runs today, who performs it, and on which system.

Raising the purchase order

Buyer · Procurement system

Policy point one states all purchase orders must be raised in the procurement system before goods are ordered. The export records po_before_order with two values: yes and no. Policy point four states that maverick spend — ordering without a purchase order — is not permitted under any circumstances.

Source: Procurement Policy and Purchase Order Export

Approval routing by value threshold

Category Manager; Finance · Procurement system

Policy point two states any purchase order above EUR 50,000 requires approval from the Category Manager and a second approval from Finance before it is released to the supplier. The export records two distinct approval_status values: approved and single_approval_only.

Source: Procurement Policy and Purchase Order Export

Second approval evidence

Finance · Procurement system

The export lists seven single_approval_only POs at amounts 54000, 61000, 67000, 72000, 88000, 95000, 120000 — all above 50000 — totalling EUR 557,000, equal to 12 percent of total PO value. The export second-approval note reads count zero, no matching lines.

Source: Procurement Policy and Purchase Order Export

Maverick spend records

Buyer · Procurement system

The export records three POs with po_before_order equal to no, totalling EUR 216,000, equal to 4.7 percent of total PO value. All three of these POs also carry the single_approval_only status, restated verbatim from the export as po_before_order=no & single_approval_only: count 3, sum 216000.

Source: Procurement Policy and Purchase Order Export
