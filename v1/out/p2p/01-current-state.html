Current State Assessment

How Procure-to-Pay runs today at Acme Manufacturing. A factual baseline of the process, the systems that support it, and how information is structured — stated as fact, no judgements.

1Domain overview

1.1Context and scope

This section documents the current state of the procurement-to-order process as evidenced by the procurement policy and the purchase order export. The policy defines four control requirements: all purchase orders must be raised in the procurement system before goods are ordered; any PO above EUR 50,000 requires approval from the Category Manager and a second approval from Finance before release to the supplier; POs are matched against the goods receipt and the supplier invoice (three-way match); and ordering without a purchase order (maverick spend) is not permitted. The purchase order export is the transactional record of POs raised, capturing the supplier, category, amount, the buyer who raised it, approval status, and whether the PO preceded the order.

Purchase orders are raised by named buyers in the procurement system and progress through approval before release to the supplier. The policy requires PO creation prior to ordering, a dual approval for orders above EUR 50,000, and a three-way match against goods receipt and supplier invoice. The export carries seven columns (po_id, supplier, category, amount_eur, raised_by, approval_status, po_before_order); it does not contain a goods receipt or invoice column. Across the recorded population, 7 POs are flagged single_approval_only carrying EUR 557,000 (12% of total PO value), 3 POs were ordered without a prior PO carrying EUR 216,000 (4.7% of total PO value), and EUR 4,625,800 of PO value is without match evidence.
